| Symbolism | Wind blowing over earth — observing everything |

The hexagram name itself (觀 = Observation) directly corresponds to the nature of an experiment.

### Ti-Yong Analysis

| Role | Trigram | Element | Represents |
|------|---------|---------|------------|
| 體 (Ti) | 巽 ☴ | Wood | The experimenter / the skill |
| 用 (Yong) | 坤 ☷ | Earth | The betting market / test environment |

**Relationship**: 體克用 (Ti controls Yong) — **Auspicious**

Wood controls Earth. The experimenter has control over the experiment. Favorable for conducting this test.

### Mutual Hexagram: 山地剝 #23 (Splitting Apart)

| Aspect | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 剝 | Stripping away, peeling, decay |
| Warning | Expect losses along the way |
| Positive spin | Illusions will be stripped away — truth revealed |

**Interpretation**: The experiment WILL have losing predictions. This is expected and healthy — it strips away false assumptions and reveals the true signal (or lack thereof).

### Transformed Hexagram: 風水渙 #59 (Dispersion)

| Aspect | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 渙 | Scattering, dispersing, spreading |
| Outcome | Results will spread and be shared |
| Implication | Worth publishing regardless of outcome |

**Interpretation**: The experiment results are meant to be disseminated. Whether Meihua proves predictive or not, the findings should be shared openly.

### Line 2 Text

> 窺觀，利女貞。
> "Peeping observation. Advantageous for a woman's perseverance."

Traditional interpretation: Observing from a limited perspective.

**For this experiment**: Start with a narrow, focused test (limited markets, clear criteria). Don't try to observe everything at once.
