EVENT: Adobe Summit 2026 Keynote (Apr 19-22)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 50 火風鼎 (Ding / The Cauldron) -> CHANGED: 56 火山旅 (Lu / The Wanderer) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The image is of a great bronze cauldron set over a wood fire -- flame above, wind feeding it from below, the ancient vessel of transformation where raw ingredients are cooked into something refined and nourishing. Fire (Li) sits atop Wind/Wood (Xun), and the body trigram (Li/Fire) is fed by the function trigram (Xun/Wood), since Wood generates Fire. This is a structure of active nourishment: the function pours its energy into the body, stoking it to brilliance. The Cauldron is the hexagram of civilizational renewal, of taking what is old and recasting it into new form. The entire image vibrates with purposeful transformation.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Li (Fire) represents clarity, illumination, and display. It is the visible, radiant element that the audience sees. Fire is being actively fed by Wood, meaning the supporting infrastructure is pouring resources into the presentation.
- The function trigram Xun (Wood/Wind) represents gentle penetration, preparation, and the invisible labor that feeds the visible flame. Wood generates Fire in the five-element cycle, so the function actively empowers the body -- a highly favorable configuration.
- The second line, the moving line, reads: "鼎有實，我仇有疾，不我能即，吉" -- "The cauldron has substance, my rival is afflicted and cannot approach me, auspicious." This places the event in a position of substantive strength where competitors are unable to challenge effectively.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The cauldron is already filled with real substance. The second line's message is one of confident advantage: the vessel contains genuine offerings, and those who might oppose or rival this effort are weakened, unable to close the distance. The transformation into The Wanderer (Lu, #56) is revealing -- from the stable, nourishing cauldron, the energy shifts toward travel, impermanence, and movement across unfamiliar terrain. Fire now sits above Mountain rather than Wind, suggesting that the illumination detaches from its fuel source and must find new ground. The Wanderer's dictum is "small success" -- not grand triumph but modest, mobile achievement. This arc moves from a position of concentrated, well-resourced strength toward a phase of dispersal and journeying outward.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries a dual flavor: initial abundance and substantive power giving way to a lighter, more mobile phase. There is the satisfying heaviness of a full cauldron -- real content, genuine innovation, competitors held at bay -- followed by the bittersweet quality of the traveler who must leave the hearth and carry the flame into the wider world. The feeling is of something being launched from a position of strength into uncertain but promising territory.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A bronze cauldron filled with rich offerings, set over a blazing wood fire -- substance being transformed by sustained energy.
- A rival afflicted and unable to approach -- competitive advantage secured not by aggression but by the sheer quality of what is in the vessel.
- The traveler departing the warm hearth, carrying fire into unfamiliar mountains -- innovation leaving its origin to seek adoption elsewhere.
- Wood feeding flame -- invisible infrastructure generating visible brilliance.
- The shift from cauldron to wanderer -- from creation to dissemination.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise lies in the transformation: what begins as a powerful, stable, well-resourced display does not remain stationary. The Wanderer hexagram warns that fire on the mountain burns bright but moves on quickly -- it does not settle. Whatever is unveiled or announced here will need to travel, to find new homes, to prove itself in unfamiliar contexts. The second line's confidence about rivals being unable to approach is strong, but the shift to Lu suggests that this advantage is time-limited. The counsel of The Wanderer is to remain humble and careful while in transit -- "旅貞吉," the traveler who stays correct finds fortune. Overconfidence born from the cauldron's fullness could become liability once the journey begins.
