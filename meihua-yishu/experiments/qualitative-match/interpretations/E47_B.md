EVENT: Chevron Championship Women's Golf (Apr 23-26)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 56 火山旅 (Lu / The Wanderer) -> CHANGED: 35 火地晉 (Jin / Progress) (Line 3)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Fire sits atop a mountain -- a conspicuous blaze on high ground, visible from great distances but impermanent, always moving on. The Wanderer is the hexagram of those far from home, operating in borrowed spaces, carrying only what they can manage. The body trigram is Li (Fire), representing clarity, brilliance, and illumination, while the function trigram is Gen (Mountain/Earth). Fire generates Earth in the five-element cycle, meaning the body produces and feeds the function -- the traveler's energy nourishes the terrain she passes through, but she herself is drained in the process. The fire gives its light to the mountain and receives nothing in return.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body (Li/Fire) is the visible, luminous force -- talent on display, brilliance that draws the eye. But Fire generating Earth means the body expends itself supporting the ground beneath it, an unfavorable expenditure.
- The function (Gen/Mountain/Earth) absorbs the fire's energy passively. The mountain stands unchanged while the fire burns and moves on. The environment takes more than it gives.
- The third line reads: "旅焚其次，喪其童僕，貞厲" -- "The traveler burns her lodging, loses her servants, and correctness brings danger." This is the most violent line in the hexagram: the place of rest is destroyed, support is lost, and even right behavior does not protect against peril.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The journey takes a harsh turn at the third line. The lodging burns -- the temporary refuge is destroyed, whether by accident or by the traveler's own fire spreading beyond control. The servants are lost -- the support network crumbles. "貞厲" warns that even doing the right thing is dangerous in this position. The third line sits at the top of the lower trigram, a position traditionally fraught with peril and excess. The transformation into Progress (Jin, #35) offers a dramatic reversal. Fire now sits above Earth (not Mountain), and the image shifts to "康侯用錫馬蕃庶，晝日三接" -- a lord being granted horses and received three times in a single day. From burning lodgings and lost servants, the scene transforms into rapid, honored advancement.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of crisis-born advancement. The destruction at the third line -- the burning, the loss -- is not the end but the catalyst. From the ashes of the traveler's misfortune rises a new trajectory of genuine progress and recognition. There is the quality of someone who loses everything on the road and yet emerges into daylight with new honors. The emotional arc is harrowing before it becomes triumphant, and the triumph tastes of survival rather than easy victory.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Fire on a mountaintop, blazing and visible but consuming itself -- brilliance that cannot sustain its own position.
- A lodging burned down in the night -- the traveler's temporary refuge destroyed, the safety net gone.
- Lost servants -- the support structure that was supposed to carry one through the journey dissolving.
- A lord received three times in a single day with gifts of horses -- the sudden, accelerating recognition that follows the darkness.
- The shift from mountain to flat earth beneath the fire -- from precarious high ground to the broad plain of progress.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The primary warning is the violence of the third line. "旅焚其次" is unambiguous: something burns down. The support system fails, the resting place is destroyed, and danger accompanies even correct behavior. The surprise is that this destruction leads not to further decline but to Progress -- Jin is a genuinely positive hexagram of advancement and public recognition. The element dynamic of Fire generating Earth means the body is constantly giving away its energy, which explains the exhaustion and loss at line three. But the transformation suggests that this expenditure, however painful, is not wasted -- it clears the ground for the rapid advance that follows. The key insight is that the traveler must not cling to what burns; the ashes become the foundation for progress.
