EVENT: Cygnus NG-24 ISS Resupply Launch (Apr 9)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 48 水風井 (Jing / The Well) -> CHANGED: 39 水山蹇 (Jian / Obstruction) (Line 2)
体用: 體=坎(水), 用=巽(木). 用生體, 體方受益.

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Water above wind (wood): the image of the Well, a structure that draws nourishment from deep below the surface and delivers it upward for communal use. "改邑不改井" -- cities may change but the well does not. This hexagram speaks of an enduring resource, a lifeline that serves regardless of who governs or what era prevails. The five-element relationship is nurturing: wood (巽) generates water (坎), meaning the foundational infrastructure actively feeds and sustains the principal element. The situation describes a deep, reliable source being tapped through patient, methodical effort.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The second line -- "井谷射鮒，甕敝漏" -- the well valley shoots at minnows, the jug is cracked and leaking. This is a troubling image: the well exists but its delivery mechanism is faulty. Water is present at the source but cannot reach those who need it because the vessel is damaged. The transformation into Obstruction (蹇) -- water above mountain -- confirms that the path from source to destination encounters significant impediment. The key tension is between abundant supply and flawed conveyance.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with a functional well -- resources exist, the source is deep and reliable, and the supporting infrastructure (wood generating water) is inherently sound. But the second line introduces a critical flaw in the delivery system: the container leaks, the mechanism misfires. Instead of nourishing water rising smoothly, the effort catches only minnows -- trivial returns from a system capable of much more. The transformation into Obstruction sharpens this: the path forward encounters a mountain, and water pools behind the barrier rather than flowing freely. The narrative is of a fundamentally sound resource encountering a mechanical or logistical failure that prevents full delivery.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of frustration amid adequacy -- the well is there, the water is there, but something in the chain of delivery breaks down. It tastes of a cracked vessel, of potential unrealized not because the source is lacking but because the means of transport falters. There is obstruction where there should be flow.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A cracked jug at a deep well -- the source is reliable but the vessel cannot hold.
- Shooting at minnows in the well valley -- effort expended on trivial catches when the real resource lies deeper.
- Water pooling against a mountain -- flow interrupted by an immovable obstacle.
- "改邑不改井" -- the permanent resource that outlasts any particular attempt to use it.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is in the severity of the second line's image: the well itself is not the problem, the delivery mechanism is. This suggests that whatever goes wrong is not a failure of purpose or resource but of the practical apparatus -- the vessel, the conduit, the equipment. The transformation into Obstruction (蹇) doubles down on this warning: "利西南，不利東北" -- some directions are favorable, others are not, and the obstacle is real. The warning is that even with a sound foundation and supportive conditions, a flawed component in the chain can prevent the mission from completing as intended.
