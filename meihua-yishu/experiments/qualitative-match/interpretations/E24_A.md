EVENT: UN/INTERPOL Global Fraud Summit
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 26 山天大畜 (Da Xu / Great Accumulation) -> CHANGED: 41 山澤損 (Sun / Decrease) (Line 3)
MUTUAL: 54 雷澤歸妹 (Gui Mei / The Marrying Maiden)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Da Xu is the image of the mountain containing heaven -- enormous creative power held in check by the stillness of the mountain. This is the hexagram of great restraint, of accumulated strength, resources, and wisdom stored up over time. It speaks to institutions that have gathered vast reserves of knowledge and capability, now holding them in readiness. In the context of a global fraud summit, this hexagram paints a picture of powerful organizations marshaling their collective resources -- intelligence databases, forensic expertise, legal frameworks -- all stockpiled and waiting to be deployed. The mountain sits atop heaven's creative force, suggesting that what is being restrained is nothing less than the full power of international cooperation.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The mountain (Gen) above represents the restraining institutional framework -- the summit itself as a structure designed to channel and focus accumulated power. It is the still, watchful presence that determines when and how force is deployed.
- Heaven (Qian) below is raw creative and executive power -- the investigative and enforcement capabilities of participating nations, straining to be unleashed.
- The third line, where the change occurs, represents a critical transition point: the moment when accumulated energy begins to move outward.
- The mutual hexagram Gui Mei (Marrying Maiden) suggests that beneath the surface of this great accumulation, there are asymmetric partnerships -- alliances where one party holds significantly more power than the other, and arrangements that are more political than organic.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The hexagram begins with the image of vast reserves held in check. The third line reads: "良馬逐，利艱貞。曰閑輿衛，利有攸往" -- "Fine horses give chase; favorable to persevere through difficulty. Daily practice with chariots and defense; favorable to have a direction to go." This is the moment the accumulated force begins to move. The imagery is military and purposeful: well-bred horses trained for pursuit, daily drills with war chariots. It speaks directly to the disciplined mobilization of resources -- law enforcement agencies that have trained, prepared, and now launch coordinated operations. The transformation into Sun (Decrease) is telling. After the mobilization comes a deliberate reduction or sacrifice. Sun is not about loss through misfortune but about strategic decrease -- giving up something at the lower level to benefit the higher. The third line of Sun reads: "三人行，則損一人；一人行，則得其友" -- "Three walk together, one is lost; one walks alone, then gains a friend." This suggests that the collaborative effort requires streamlining: too many actors dilute effectiveness, and a focused, singular approach attracts genuine allies. The mutual hexagram Gui Mei indicates that the internal dynamics of cooperation involve unequal partnerships and political arrangements -- not all parties at the summit table hold equivalent stakes or contribute equally.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome is characterized by disciplined reduction after initial abundance. The flavor is austere and strategic: having gathered everything possible, the process now demands selective sacrifice and focused deployment. It is the quality of a surgeon choosing the single precise incision rather than the general who deploys every battalion. There is effectiveness in this decrease, but also a certain severity -- not everything accumulated will be used, and some partnerships will be trimmed for the sake of operational clarity.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Fine horses trained for pursuit suddenly released -- coordinated law enforcement operations launched after long preparation.
- A mountain containing the sky -- the sheer scale of accumulated international intelligence and resources, held under institutional control.
- Three travelers becoming two -- the necessary reduction of actors to achieve genuine partnership.
- Daily chariot drills at dawn -- the disciplined, repetitive preparation that precedes effective action.
- A younger sister married into a great household she did not choose -- the asymmetric power dynamics within international coalitions.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise lies in the transition from accumulation to decrease. One might expect a summit designed to pool resources to result in greater collective power, but the hexagram suggests the opposite trajectory: the process of coming together actually requires strategic reduction. The third line's emphasis on "艱貞" (persevering through difficulty) warns that mobilization is not effortless -- the fine horses chase, but the terrain is hard. The mutual hexagram Gui Mei adds an undercurrent of political discomfort: some arrangements forged at this summit may feel more like obligatory marriages than genuine alliances, and the unequal distribution of effort and benefit could become a source of tension beneath the surface of cooperation.
