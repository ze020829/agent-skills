EVENT: Michael (MJ Biopic) Opening Weekend (Apr 17)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 38 火澤睽 (Kui / Opposition) -> CHANGED: 10 天澤履 (Lu / Treading) (Line 5)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Fire above and lake below -- two elements that move in opposite directions, flame rising and water sinking, creating an image of fundamental divergence and opposition. Kui is the hexagram of estrangement, of seeing the world through suspicious eyes, of forces that should cooperate but find themselves at cross-purposes. Yet the hexagram text says "小事吉" -- small matters are auspicious. Within this atmosphere of discord, something modest can still succeed. The fifth line, at the sovereign position, offers a specific pathway through the opposition.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The Ti trigram is Dui (metal/lake), the subject -- articulate, joyful on the surface, associated with expression and public appeal. The Yong trigram is Li (fire), the external environment -- brilliant, illuminating, attention-commanding. Fire overcomes metal (火克金): Yong克Ti, meaning the external environment exerts a melting, refining pressure on the subject. The subject is under scrutiny, being tested by the very brilliance that surrounds it. The fifth line occupies the position of leadership within the fire trigram, the eye of the scrutinizing gaze.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The fifth line reads: "悔亡，厥宗噬膚，往何咎." Regret vanishes. The clan bites through the skin -- going forward, what blame could there be? This is a breakthrough moment within the opposition: a close ally or kindred spirit penetrates the surface barrier, making genuine contact possible. The image of "biting through skin" suggests that initial resistance gives way, and what appeared to be estrangement reveals itself as merely a superficial membrane. The transformation into Hexagram 10, Lu (Treading), introduces the famous image of treading on a tiger's tail without being bitten -- an audacious act that succeeds through proper conduct and careful positioning.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of a bold venture that succeeds against expectation. There is dazzle in it -- the fire element ensures visibility and spectacle -- but also the delicate tension of walking a tightrope. Lu's "履虎尾，不咥人" promises that the dangerous step will not result in harm, but the very metaphor acknowledges how close to danger the entire enterprise runs. The resolution feels like applause following a high-wire act: exhilarating, slightly breathless, genuinely impressive but never quite comfortable.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Fire and lake pulling apart -- two brilliant surfaces facing opposite directions, each reflecting differently.
- Biting through skin to reach the flesh -- penetrating initial resistance to find genuine substance beneath.
- Treading on a tiger's tail -- an act of supreme audacity that depends entirely on grace and propriety.
- A clan reunited across estrangement -- what appeared to be opposition dissolving into recognition.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The critical warning is fire's dominance over metal: the external environment is structurally overwhelming to the subject, and the pressure of scrutiny and brilliance may be intense. The fifth line's "悔亡" (regret vanishes) is the surprise -- within a hexagram of opposition, regret disappears precisely at the moment of genuine contact. Lu's warning is embedded in its image: the tiger's tail is always dangerous, and the fact that one is not bitten does not mean the tiger is tame. Success here depends on impeccable conduct under extreme pressure, not on the absence of risk.
