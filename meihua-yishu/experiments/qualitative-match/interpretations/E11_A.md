HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 48 井 (The Well) → CHANGED: 46 升 (Ascending) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: 38 睽 (Opposition/Divergence)

Changing line text (九五): 井冽，寒泉食
("The well is clear and cold. The cold spring water can be drawn and consumed.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation centered on an enduring communal resource -- the well -- which sustains a community regardless of who governs or what political arrangements change. The well's nature is constant: "the town may be moved, but the well does not move" (改邑不改井). At the fifth line (the sovereign position), the well has reached its clearest, purest state. The energy is one of something fundamental and nourishing becoming available, though the mutual hexagram 睽 (Divergence) reveals deep division in how this resource is perceived.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The well (井) represents an enduring institutional or rhetorical resource -- something that draws from deep below and offers sustenance to all who come. At line 5, this resource is in its clearest, most accessible condition.
- Water over Wind (坎 over 巽) creates the image of water being drawn upward by the mechanism of the well. The effort is in the lifting and presenting, not in the water itself.
- The mutual hexagram 睽 (Opposition) introduces a fundamental dynamic of divergence: two parties looking in opposite directions, unable to agree on what they are seeing even when looking at the same thing.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The well has been cleaned and restored (the preceding lines describe a well going from muddy and neglected to clear). At the fifth line, the purification process is complete -- the cold spring water is pure and available. This represents a moment where something essential is being offered clearly and directly. The changed hexagram 升 (Ascending) suggests that from this moment of clarity, there is upward movement -- the act of drawing pure water from the well leads to rising fortunes or rising energy. However, the mutual hexagram 睽 reveals that the internal process is one of deep division. Even when the well offers clear water, the community around it is fractured, looking in different directions, disagreeing about the well's value or the quality of its water. The turning point is the moment of drawing -- the water is there, clear and cold, but the question is who drinks and who turns away. The tension is between the objective clarity of what is being offered (井冽) and the subjective divergence in how it is received (睽).

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a quality of clarity meeting disagreement. Something essential is presented in its purest form, but the reaction is split rather than unified. The flavor is of a resource that is genuinely nourishing but contested -- people disagree not about whether the well exists, but about whether to drink from it. The changed hexagram 升 suggests that despite the division, there is net upward movement: the act of presenting something clear and fundamental does produce elevation, even if not for everyone equally. The resolution feels productive but polarized.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A deep well with crystalline cold water, perfectly restored and functional, sitting in the center of a divided town where half the residents refuse to draw from it.
- Clear, cold spring water drawn up from deep underground -- the emphasis is on purity, depth, and the labor required to bring what is below up to the surface.
- Two people standing at the same well, backs to each other, each convinced the other is wrong about the water's quality (睽).

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram warns that clarity of presentation does not guarantee unity of reception. The surprise is in the depth of the divergence (睽): the clearer and purer the offering, the more sharply divided the response becomes. Those who drink find genuine nourishment (井冽，寒泉食 is unambiguously positive), but those who refuse to drink become more entrenched in their refusal. The well does its job perfectly, but the community fractures further around it.
