PRIMARY: 58 兌為澤 (Dui / Joy) -> CHANGED: 43 澤天夬 (Guai / Breakthrough) (Line 3)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents lake upon lake -- pure, doubled joy, the image of two bodies of water reflecting each other, mouths open in conversation and delight. Dui is the trigram of pleasure, communication, and the youngest daughter. Doubled, it suggests an atmosphere saturated with festivity and mutual gratification. Yet the third line darkens this picture: "來兌，凶" -- joy that comes seeking, pleasure that arrives from outside, is ominous. This is the dangerous face of delight: pleasure pursued for its own sake, gratification that one goes out to find rather than allowing to arise naturally.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- Both body and functioning trigrams are Dui (metal), creating a situation where metal meets metal -- no generative or destructive dynamic, but also no growth or transformation through elemental interaction. The forces mirror each other perfectly, which sounds harmonious but can also indicate stagnation or an echo chamber of identical energies.
- The third line sits at the top of the lower trigram, a transitional and typically precarious position. Here, it represents the moment when internal joy tips over into external seeking -- the turn from genuine pleasure to its hollow pursuit.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The transformation into hexagram 43, Lake over Heaven (Guai / Breakthrough), introduces a dramatic shift. Guai is the image of five yang lines pushing out one remaining yin at the top -- a decisive, public rupture. The guaci declares "揚于王庭" -- proclaiming in the king's court. The arc moves from doubled joy and festive atmosphere to a single, definitive moment of breakthrough where something is resolved publicly and irrevocably. The third line of Guai reads: "壯于頄，有凶。君子夬夬，獨行遇雨，若濡有慍，无咎" -- strength in the face, danger; the superior person walks alone through rain, gets wet and annoyed, but ultimately no blame. This is the image of someone who must endure an unpleasant passage to reach resolution.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the flavor of festivity interrupted by a sudden, decisive event. The atmosphere of mutual delight gives way to a single sharp moment that separates winners from the field. There is the sense of walking alone through rain -- isolated, uncomfortable, perhaps unjustly criticized -- but arriving at the destination without lasting harm. The resolution is public, dramatic, and leaves no room for ambiguity.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Two lakes reflecting each other -- an atmosphere of doubled pleasure, surfaces gleaming with mutual delight.
- Pleasure that comes seeking -- the dangerous guest who arrives uninvited, bringing ominous energy beneath a smiling face.
- A proclamation in the king's court -- the public, undeniable moment when the outcome is declared for all to witness.
- Walking alone through rain -- the superior person enduring temporary discomfort and disapproval on the way to a clean resolution.
- Five yang lines expelling the last yin -- overwhelming force driving out the final holdout.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The third line's "來兌，凶" is a clear warning: pleasure or advantage that is actively pursued rather than earned brings misfortune. In a field of identical energies (metal upon metal), the danger is that everyone seeks the same thing and the competition becomes self-consuming. The surprise is the transformation into Breakthrough -- what appears to be pure festivity suddenly resolves into a stark, judicial moment of decision. The rain imagery suggests that the path to resolution is uncomfortable and isolating, even if ultimately blameless. The lesson is that genuine joy requires no pursuit, and the decisive moment will come regardless of whether one chases it.
