HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 57 巽 (The Gentle, Wind) → CHANGED: 48 井 (The Well) (Line 6)
MUTUAL: 38 睽 (Opposition)

Changing line text (上九): 巽在床下，喪其資斧，貞凶
("Penetrating beneath the bed. One loses one's resources and axe. Perseverance brings misfortune.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation of excessive, almost obsessive compliance and submissiveness. Double wind (巽 over 巽) represents penetration and gentle influence doubled, but at the top line it has gone too far — the gentle approach has become self-abasing groveling. The overall energy is one where soft diplomacy and behind-the-scenes maneuvering have reached a point of diminishing returns, and the very tools of negotiation are being lost in the process.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- A negotiating or mediating force that has bent so low in accommodation that it has lost its leverage — "penetrating beneath the bed" suggests going beneath what is dignified or effective. The resources and authority ("axe") that gave this party bargaining power are slipping away.
- The mutual hexagram 睽 (Opposition) reveals that beneath all the diplomatic courtesy, the parties fundamentally see the world in opposing ways. The surface gentleness masks an irreconcilable divergence of vision.
- The changed hexagram 井 (The Well) suggests a deep, structural resource — something immovable and essential — that outlasts the current maneuvering.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The process begins with repeated attempts at gentle persuasion and subtle influence. The wind blows and blows, but at the sixth line — the very end — the wind has exhausted itself. The changing line is stark: penetrating beneath the bed is an image of having gone too low, of submission that has become self-destructive. The "resources and axe" — the practical tools and authority needed to act — are lost.

The mutual hexagram 睽 (Opposition) tells us that throughout the middle of this process, the parties are looking at each other with suspicion and fundamental disagreement. They may sit at the same table, but they see different realities. Fire and lake (the components of 睽) evaporate each other.

The changed hexagram 井 (The Well) offers an unexpected structural insight: the well does not move. It remains in its place regardless of what happens above ground. The turning point is when the actors recognize that the deep structures (territorial realities, resource dependencies, historical grievances) are not changed by the negotiations — the well stays where it is.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome flavor is one of exhaustion and loss of position. The gentle approach does not achieve its aims; instead, the negotiating party finds itself weakened. The resolution is not dramatic collapse but a slow draining of leverage. The feeling is deeply unresolved — the well remains, the fundamental structures remain, but the immediate diplomatic effort loses its tools. There is a quality of "too much bending breaks the reed."

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Wind blowing under a bed — an image of influence that has become so diffuse it no longer moves anything, just stirs dust in hidden corners.
- A diplomat who has made so many concessions that they no longer have anything to offer or threaten with — the axe (authority to act decisively) is lost.
- A well in an abandoned village — the water is still there, the structure remains, but the community that once drew from it has scattered (井).

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram warns emphatically that continued soft accommodation at this stage will be counterproductive — "perseverance brings misfortune." The surprise element is that the enduring reality is not shaped by the current negotiations at all but by deep, well-like structures that cannot be moved by diplomatic winds, no matter how persistent.
