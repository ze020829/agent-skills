EVENT: London Marathon 2026
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 16 雷地豫 (Yu / Enthusiasm) -> CHANGED: 35 火地晉 (Jin / Progress) (Line 6)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents thunder rumbling over the earth -- a scene of collective joy and anticipation, of multitudes moving together in shared enthusiasm. The image is of a great gathering animated by a single driving rhythm, like drums beating a marching cadence that sweeps everyone forward. The earth below is receptive and yielding, while the thunder above provides the singular burst of motivating energy. Yet the moving line is at the very top, the sixth position, where the text warns: "冥豫，成有渝，无咎" -- enthusiasm carried into darkness, and once the thing is accomplished, there must be a change. This suggests that the initial euphoria reaches an unsustainable peak and must transform.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram is Kun (earth), passive, receptive, representing the broad mass -- a large collective that supports and endures. The functioning trigram is Zhen (thunder), the initiating spark of excitement. In the five-element dynamic, wood (Zhen) controls earth (Kun), meaning the energizing force dominates and somewhat drains the receptive body.
- The sixth line occupies the terminal position -- this is someone or something at the very end of the enthusiasm cycle, having pushed past the point of healthy exuberance into a kind of blind, dark revelry.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with mass enthusiasm and collective momentum. The transformation into hexagram 35, Fire over Earth (Jin / Progress), shifts the image from thunder's explosive excitement to fire's steady, ascending light -- a sunrise over flat ground. The Jin hexagram speaks of "康侯用錫馬蕃庶，晝日三接" -- a vassal lord rewarded with horses and received three times in a single day. This is the image of recognition, promotion, and visible advancement. The process therefore moves from raw collective excitement to a more measured, publicly acknowledged ascent. However, because the moving line warns of "dark enthusiasm" that must change once accomplished, there is a suggestion that the initial burst of energy is somewhat reckless or overextended, and the real progress only comes after that excess is corrected.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of advancement after initial overexertion. There is a sense of a large, joyful collective effort that nearly tips into excess but ultimately resolves into genuine upward movement. The feeling is of morning light spreading across a plain -- steady, warm, unmistakable progress visible to all. The tone shifts from boisterous drumbeats to the quieter glow of earned recognition.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder over earth -- a vast crowd moved by a single percussive rhythm, feet pounding in unison across flat terrain.
- Darkness at the peak of revelry -- the party that goes on too long, where the music plays past midnight into a kind of blind compulsion.
- A sunrise over open ground -- after the thunder fades, steady firelight rises, illuminating everything gradually.
- A vassal lord rewarded with horses -- the image of public recognition and tangible reward for endurance.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The top-line warning is stark: enthusiasm carried into darkness becomes self-destructive. The word "冥" (dark, blind) suggests that the initial energy, however thrilling, is not entirely clear-sighted. Someone or something pushes past the natural endpoint. The surprise is that despite this overreach, the text says "无咎" -- no blame, provided there is willingness to change once the thing is accomplished. The wood-controls-earth dynamic also warns that the driving force may exhaust the supporting base. The real achievement comes not from the thunder but from the quiet fire that follows.
