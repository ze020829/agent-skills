EVENT: Djibouti Presidential Election (Apr 10)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 33 天山遯 (Dun / Retreat) -> CHANGED: 44 天風姤 (Gou / Encountering) (Line 2)
体用: 體=乾(金), 用=艮(土). 用生體, 體方受益.

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Heaven above mountain: the image of Retreat, where the creative principle rises above and away from the earthly, drawing back to preserve itself as lesser forces advance below. The hexagram counsels strategic withdrawal -- "亨，小利貞" -- there is success, with small advantage in maintaining correctness. The five-element dynamic strongly favors 體: earth (艮) generates metal (乾), meaning the very ground from which retreat occurs continues to nourish and support the retreating power. This is not flight from weakness but withdrawal from strength -- a sovereign who steps back because the timing demands it, not because the resources are depleted.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The second line -- "執之用黃牛之革，莫之勝說" -- bound with yellow oxhide, impossible to loosen. This image is of absolute, unbreakable binding. Yellow is the color of the center, of earth, of loyal steadfastness. Whatever is held in place at this moment cannot be dislodged by any force. The transformation into Encountering (姤) -- heaven above wind -- introduces the unexpected meeting, a yin force entering from below. The key tension is between an immovable binding and an uninvited arrival.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The narrative begins with retreat as the prevailing strategy -- a controlled, dignified stepping back. But the second line arrests this movement entirely: the subject is bound so firmly that retreat itself becomes impossible. This is not a failure of the retreat strategy but a revelation that at this particular position, the binding to the current situation is total. The transformation into Encountering shifts the energy dramatically: from withdrawal to unexpected contact, from pulling away to a new force appearing from below. With 用 generating 體 throughout, the institutional foundations sustain the principal actor even through this surprising turn. The process moves from intended retreat to enforced staying, and then to an unforeseen encounter.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the paradoxical flavor of a retreat that never happens because the binding is too strong. What was planned as withdrawal becomes an encounter. There is something inevitable in this -- the oxhide cannot be loosened, the new arrival cannot be refused. The quality is of fate overriding intention, of being held in place for a meeting that was not sought.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Yellow oxhide binding -- the color of the loyal center, an unbreakable tether to one's position.
- Heaven retreating above a mountain -- sovereignty drawing back to high ground.
- Wind entering beneath heaven -- an uninvited force rising from below, gentle but pervasive.
- "女壯，勿用取女" (from 姤) -- a strong feminine force that should not be married, an encounter with something powerful and ungovernable.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The greatest surprise is the transformation from Retreat to Encountering -- two hexagrams with opposite energies. The intention to withdraw is overtaken by an unexpected engagement. The second line's binding suggests that despite any desire to step back, the principal actor is locked into the situation by bonds that cannot be broken. The warning from Encountering is clear: the arriving force ("女壯") is powerful and should not be underestimated or embraced carelessly. Whatever enters the scene uninvited must be managed with extreme caution.
