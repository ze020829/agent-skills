EVENT: NVIDIA GTC 2026 Jensen Huang Keynote
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 37 風火家人 (Jia Ren / The Family) -> CHANGED: 9 風天小畜 (Xiao Xu / Small Accumulation) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 64 火水未濟 (Wei Ji / Before Completion)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The situation is fundamentally domestic in character. Wind over fire -- the image of warmth radiating outward from a hearth, order emanating from within a household. Hexagram 37, Jia Ren, speaks of family governance, of roles properly assigned and relationships harmoniously maintained within a bounded community. The energy here is centripetal rather than centrifugal: the concern is with internal coherence, with making sure every member of the household understands their place and contributes to the functioning of the whole. This is not a moment of outward conquest but of inward tending.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The "family" in question is a structured community with clear hierarchical roles -- someone manages the internal economy while others carry out their designated functions. There is a strong feminine or nurturing principle at work: the second line specifically addresses the one who manages the household provisions.
- The mutual hexagram Wei Ji (Before Completion) introduces a powerful undercurrent of incompleteness. Despite the orderly appearance of the household, something fundamental remains unresolved -- the fox has not yet crossed the river, and its tail risks getting wet. There is a gap between current arrangements and final success.
- The changed hexagram Xiao Xu (Small Accumulation) suggests that the outcome involves only a modest gathering of resources -- clouds forming but rain not yet falling. The capacity to fully realize what is intended remains just out of reach.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The narrative begins within the warmth and structure of the family circle. Jia Ren sets the stage: "利女貞" -- the situation favors those who embody steady devotion and attentive management. The changing second line reads: "无攸遂，在中饋，貞吉" -- there is nothing to be accomplished through assertive external action; rather, one should attend to the inner provisions, the central sustenance of the household.守正則吉 -- maintaining correctness in this nurturing role brings good fortune. This is counsel of restraint and internal focus: do not reach outward, but tend what is within. The mutual hexagram Wei Ji then reveals that beneath this orderly surface, the situation is fundamentally incomplete -- the crossing has not yet been made, the final resolution remains ahead. Things look arranged but are not yet settled. The transformation into Xiao Xu confirms this: the wind blows across heaven, clouds gather in the western suburbs, but rain does not fall. Accumulation is small, partial, promising but not yet delivering.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the flavor of careful domestic management that produces only modest results. There is warmth, structure, and sincerity, but the grand promise remains unfulfilled. The feeling is of tending a hearth that gives comfort to those nearby but whose light does not carry far. Something important is being nurtured, but it has not yet grown large enough to matter beyond the walls of the household. The aftertaste is one of competent stewardship awaiting a larger moment.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A woman tending the kitchen, preparing provisions for the household -- diligent, essential, but unseen by the wider world.
- Dense clouds massing over the western suburbs but withholding their rain -- potential suspended, not yet released.
- A small fox crossing an icy river, its tail dipping into the water at the last moment -- the crossing nearly complete but compromised at the final step.
- Wind feeding a hearth fire -- the bellows of communication sustaining the warmth of an inner circle.
- A household where everyone knows their role but the harvest has not yet come in.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is how inward-facing and contained this hexagram configuration is for an event that might be expected to project outward power. The emphasis falls entirely on internal management, on tending provisions rather than conquering new territory. The warning from Wei Ji is that the appearance of order should not be mistaken for completion -- the river has not been crossed, and premature confidence could lead to the fox wetting its tail. The second line's counsel to stay within the domestic sphere and not pursue external ambitions suggests that overreach would be the primary danger.
