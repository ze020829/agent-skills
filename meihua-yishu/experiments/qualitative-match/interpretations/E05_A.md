EVENT: Iran US Nuclear Crisis Deal or Strike
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 52 艮 (Keeping Still, Mountain) → CHANGED: 18 蠱 (Decay/Correcting) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 3 屯 (Difficulty at the Beginning)

Changing line text (六二): 艮其腓，不拯其隨，其心不快
("Keeping still at the calves. One cannot rescue those who follow. The heart is not glad.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation of enforced stillness and immobility. Double mountain (艮 over 艮) signifies two immovable forces facing each other, neither willing to budge. The energy is one of profound stasis — not peaceful rest, but a tense standoff where movement is suppressed rather than resolved. The atmosphere is heavy with the weight of restraint.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- Two mountain-like forces locked in opposition, each holding its ground with obstinate firmness. Neither side possesses the capacity or willingness to yield, creating a frozen tableau.
- The changing line at position 2 indicates a subordinate or mid-level actor who wants to help but cannot — "one cannot rescue those who follow." There is a sense of helpless frustration among intermediaries or allied parties.
- The mutual hexagram 屯 (Difficulty at the Beginning) reveals that beneath the surface stillness lies chaotic, embryonic energy — the situation is not truly calm but rather a pressurized state where movement has not yet found its channel.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins and remains in a state of profound immobility. Both sides are like mountains — unyielding, massive, and deeply rooted in their positions. The second-line dynamic reveals that those caught in the middle (mediators, allies, secondary parties) feel the frustration of being unable to influence the outcome: "the heart is not glad." They see the danger but cannot pull others back from the brink.

The mutual hexagram 屯 tells us that this stillness is not permanent — it is the kind of frozen moment before a thunderstorm, where water and thunder gather beneath the earth. The tension is generative but also dangerous. The changed hexagram 蠱 (Decay/Correcting) suggests that the eventual movement will be about addressing long-accumulated rot and dysfunction — old problems that have been left to fester.

The turning point comes when the stillness finally breaks, but the breaking is not triumphant. It is corrective surgery on old wounds. The resolution, if it comes, will be about cleaning up inherited messes rather than achieving something new.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome flavor is one of frustrated stagnation that eventually gives way to a slow, unglamorous process of repair. It is neither dramatic nor sudden — more like the gradual acknowledgment that old structures have decayed and need tending. The feeling is deeply unresolved in the near term; whatever emerges will feel incomplete and unsatisfying to the parties involved, particularly to those in supportive roles who wanted to do more but could not.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Two mountains facing each other across a valley — immense, unmoving, and casting long shadows. Nothing crosses between them.
- A person grabbing at someone's legs trying to hold them back, but unable to gain purchase — the calves slip through the hands.
- A house with rotting beams discovered only after the walls stop shaking — the stillness reveals the decay underneath (蠱).

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram warns that the greatest danger is not in the confrontation itself but in what the prolonged stillness allows to fester unseen. The surprise element is that when movement finally comes, it will not be about the main standoff at all — it will be about correcting old, neglected problems (蠱) that the crisis merely exposed.
