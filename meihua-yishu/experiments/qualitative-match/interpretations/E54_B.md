EVENT: Mortal Kombat 2 Opening Weekend (May 15)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 21 火雷噬嗑 (Shi He / Biting Through) -> CHANGED: 35 火地晉 (Jin / Progress) (Line 1)
BODY=離(火), FUNCTION=震(木)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The image is of jaws biting down on an obstacle -- fire above, thunder below, lightning illuminating what the teeth must crush. Shi He is the hexagram of forceful resolution, of breaking through obstructions by direct, even violent means. Something stands between the upper and lower jaws and must be bitten through before unity can be restored. The structure vibrates with aggressive, penetrating energy: thunder shakes from below while fire illuminates from above, leaving the obstacle nowhere to hide. The first line's movement transforms thunder (Zhen) into earth (Kun), yielding Jin (Progress/Advancement), the image of the sun rising over the earth -- a brilliant, visible ascent.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Li (Fire) is fueled by the function trigram Zhen (Wood). Wood feeds fire in the five-element cycle, meaning the active force directly energizes and strengthens the body. This is a powerfully generative relationship -- the source material burns bright.
- The first line is the lowest position, representing the very beginning of the process. Its yao text in Shi He speaks of initial, minor punishment -- "feet in stocks, toes cut off" -- small corrective pain that prevents larger disaster.
- Jin (Progress) as the changed hexagram introduces a fundamentally optimistic trajectory: the vassal lord (康侯) receives horses in abundance and is received three times in a single day.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The first line reads: "屨校滅趾，无咎" -- "Wearing stocks that destroy the toes, no blame." This is a small, sharp punishment at the outset -- a painful but ultimately beneficial correction that establishes boundaries early. The image is of someone who stumbles at the very first step, receives a swift corrective blow, and is thereby saved from greater error. The transformation into Jin (Progress) then opens a dramatically upward trajectory. The sun rises over the earth; the obscure figure is suddenly visible, recognized, advanced. The narrative arc moves from initial stumbling and correction to bright, public ascension. The early pain is the price of later visibility.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of dramatic reversal -- from constriction to expansion, from darkness to blazing daylight. It tastes of vindication after initial struggle, of the sun finally clearing the horizon after a long night. There is something both visceral and triumphant about this arc: the teeth bite through the obstacle, and once it is shattered, the way opens to rapid, visible progress. Jin's character is one of warmth, generosity, and public recognition -- the vassal lord showered with gifts and audiences.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Jaws clamping down on gristle -- the raw, physical act of biting through an obstruction.
- Stocks on the feet -- a sharp, early punishment that teaches respect for boundaries.
- The sun rising over flat earth -- sudden, unmistakable illumination after darkness.
- A vassal lord receiving horses and threefold audience -- public recognition, material reward, rapid advancement.
- Lightning and thunder preceding sunrise -- the violent clearing that makes way for a brilliant dawn.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the speed of the reversal from punishment to progress. The first line's stocks and severed toes seem harsh, but the transformation into Jin suggests this initial pain is precisely what catalyzes the ascent. The warning from Shi He is that the biting-through must be genuine -- half-measures leave the obstruction intact. Jin's own caution (the fourth line's "鼫鼠" image, the hoarding rat) warns against greedily clinging to gains once progress begins. The initial fire is strong and well-fed, but it must be directed rather than allowed to consume indiscriminately.
