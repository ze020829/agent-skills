HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 53 漸 (Gradual Progress) → CHANGED: 39 蹇 (Obstruction) (Line 6)
MUTUAL: 64 未濟 (Not Yet Completed)

Changing line text (上九): 鴻漸于陸，其羽可用為儀，吉
("The wild goose gradually advances to the high plateau. Its feathers can be used for ceremonial display. Auspicious.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents a situation where a long, measured process of advancement reaches its topmost point. The wild goose has completed every stage of its gradual ascent and now stands at the highest plateau, where its beauty is on full display. The overall energy is orderly, hierarchical, and ceremonial -- things arrive at their positions through established progressions rather than chaotic leaps.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The ascending goose represents entities that have climbed through a sequential process -- each stage earned, each position the result of prior stages. At line 6 (the terminal position), this process reaches its natural conclusion.
- Wind over Mountain (巽 over 艮) creates a dynamic where flexible, penetrating influence works upon solid, immovable structure. The structure does not change easily, but the persistent wind shapes perception of it.
- The mutual hexagram 未濟 (Not Yet Completed) reveals that the ceremony of sorting and placing is itself a beginning, not an ending -- the real contest or challenge has not yet been engaged.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation unfolds with a deliberate, staged quality. Each element is placed according to a logic that has been building over weeks or months. The line 6 position indicates that this ordering process reaches its peak -- the final arrangement is made, the last piece falls into place. The "feathers used for ceremony" (其羽可用為儀) emphasizes that the result of this process is primarily symbolic and display-oriented: it creates a formal structure that is beautiful to behold. However, the changed hexagram 蹇 (Obstruction) signals that immediately after this elegant arrangement, the path forward becomes difficult. What was smooth in its ascent becomes rough in its descent. The turning point is the completion of the display itself -- once everything is revealed and in position, the real difficulties begin. The mutual hexagram 未濟 underscores that this is a threshold moment: completion of one phase that opens onto something still unfinished.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome is orderly, hierarchical, and visually satisfying in its arrangement, but carries a pronounced sense of "this is only the beginning." The flavor is ceremonial and anticipatory rather than conclusive. There is elegance in the structure revealed, but the undercurrent of 未濟 means the structure contains imbalances that will become apparent later. The resolution feels partial -- everything is in its place, but some placements will prove problematic.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A flock of geese arriving at their final formation on a high plateau, each in its designated position -- orderly, visible, and ritualistic.
- Feathers arranged for display -- the emphasis is on the aesthetic and symbolic arrangement rather than on the substance of what comes next.
- A staircase that has been climbed to the top landing, only to reveal that the door at the top opens onto an icy, difficult mountain path (蹇).

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram suggests that the process of arrangement and revelation will proceed with more order and predictability than expected -- there will be few genuine shocks in the sorting itself. The surprise lies in what the arrangement reveals about the difficulties ahead: the changed hexagram 蹇 indicates that the configuration produced by this gradual process will create notably difficult paths for some, and the "obstruction" will become apparent almost immediately after the display is complete.
