EVENT: World Baseball Classic 2026 Pool Stage
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 13 同人 (Fellowship) → CHANGED: 49 革 (Revolution) (Line 6)
MUTUAL: 44 姤 (Coming to Meet)

Changing line text (上九): 同人于郊，无悔
("Fellowship in the open countryside. No regret.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a gathering of kindred spirits that begins with high ideals of fellowship and unity but arrives at a point of transformation by the end. Heaven over Fire (乾 over 離) — fire rises toward heaven, symbolizing the natural upward aspiration of like-minded people coming together. The energy is expansive and outward-looking, a community united by shared purpose. However, at the top line, the fellowship has moved to the outskirts — "the countryside" — suggesting it has reached its outer limit.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- A fellowship or alliance that has gathered with shared purpose. The 同人 hexagram emphasizes collective identity and cooperation — people drawn together across differences. The fire and heaven combination suggests brightness, ambition, and a shared vision that lifts everyone.
- The sixth line (上九) represents the outermost periphery of this fellowship — those who are connected but at the margins. "Fellowship in the countryside" means the alliance extends to the widest circle but with the least intensity. There is no regret, but also no deep engagement.
- The mutual hexagram 姤 (Coming to Meet) reveals an unexpected encounter operating beneath the surface — something or someone arrives uninvited, a chance meeting that disrupts expectations. In 姤, a single yin line enters from below five yang lines — the unexpected feminine/receptive element infiltrates the strong.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation starts with the warmth and brightness of shared endeavor — people coming together with common cause. The fire of 離 burns upward toward the vastness of 乾 (heaven), suggesting grand aspirations and a sense that "we are all in this together." The fellowship is genuine.

But the sixth line marks the end of this phase. The fellowship reaches the countryside — the farthest point from the center. This is not betrayal or failure; it is simply the natural attenuation of cohesion at the edges. "No regret" means the journey was worthwhile even if the bond thins at the margins.

The changed hexagram 革 (Revolution) is striking: from fellowship to revolution. This tells us the gathering is not static — it transforms into something that demands fundamental change. 革 is Lake over Fire — the two elements actively destroy each other (water extinguishes fire, fire evaporates water). The mutual hexagram 姤 warns of an unexpected encounter that catalyzes this transformation during the middle phase.

The turning point is when the outward-looking fellowship at the margins encounters something unexpected (姤) and the entire dynamic shifts from cooperation to transformation (革).

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a quality of transformation rather than simple conclusion. What begins as a friendly gathering evolves into something that reshapes the participants. The flavor is one of discovery at the edges — the most interesting developments happen not at the center of the fellowship but at its periphery, in the countryside. The resolution is not warm and fuzzy but revolutionary (革) — structures that seemed natural are challenged and changed. The emotional register shifts from camaraderie to something more charged and consequential.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A bonfire at the edge of a field where travelers from different places gather — the fellowship is real but temporary, and the fire itself eventually demands new fuel or burns out.
- Allies meeting in open countryside, away from walls and structure — the absence of formal boundaries means the relationship is flexible but also lacks definition.
- A molting animal (革 literally means "hide/skin") — the fellowship sheds its old form and emerges as something different, the way a snake leaves its skin behind.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram warns that the fellowship, while genuine, should not be mistaken for permanence — it is a phase that will transform into something else entirely. The surprise is the appearance of an unexpected element (姤) during the process that catalyzes a revolution (革) nobody anticipated when the gathering first formed.
