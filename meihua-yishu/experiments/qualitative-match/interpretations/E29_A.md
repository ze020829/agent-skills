EVENT: Peru Presidential Election
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 41 山澤損 (Sun / Decrease) -> CHANGED: 26 山天大畜 (Da Xu / Great Accumulation) (Line 3)
MUTUAL: 24 地雷復 (Fu / Return)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The primary hexagram Sun (Decrease) presents a situation where reduction is taking place -- something is being diminished below to benefit what is above. Mountain sits over lake: the mountain grows taller while the lake diminishes beneath it. In a presidential election context, this paints a picture of sacrifice and redistribution, where the populace (below) gives up something -- trust, resources, patience -- to elevate leadership (above). The hexagram's counsel is that decrease done with sincerity is supremely auspicious, suggesting that the essential dynamic of this election involves a population willing to accept loss or austerity in exchange for a promise of future gain.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The mountain above the lake represents an authority that accumulates while the base erodes. The winning political force may be one that openly acknowledges sacrifice or demands austerity from the people, yet frames this as necessary and virtuous.
- The mutual hexagram Fu (Return) introduces a powerful cyclical theme: after reaching the lowest point, the first stirring of renewal appears. One yang line returns at the very bottom -- this is the winter solstice of politics, the moment when decline bottoms out and a new cycle begins. It suggests the election occurs at or near a turning point in the country's fortunes.
- The changed hexagram Da Xu (Great Accumulation) represents stored power, accumulated wisdom, and restrained strength -- mountain over heaven, containing enormous creative energy within firm boundaries.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The election begins under the sign of Decrease: the nation is in a state of diminishment, resources are strained, and the electorate is being asked to sacrifice. The third line of Sun reads: "三人行，則損一人；一人行，則得其友" -- "When three walk together, one is decreased; when one walks alone, he finds his friend." This is a profound statement about coalition and solitude in politics. It suggests that a crowded field (three candidates, three factions) must lose one member to find equilibrium, while a solitary figure who strikes out alone will paradoxically attract a natural ally. The political process involves winnowing: coalitions that are too large fracture, while a lone figure who dares to stand apart discovers unexpected partnership. Through the mutual hexagram Fu (Return), we see the deeper rhythm: this election is fundamentally about return -- the return of a political force, an ideology, or a figure from the past. The single yang line at the base of Fu says "not far, return" -- whatever was lost has not traveled far and is now coming back. The resolution into Da Xu (Great Accumulation) is remarkable: the energy that was scattered and diminished is gathered and stored. The outcome is one of consolidation and containment -- great power held within firm institutional boundaries, like taming wild horses or accumulating virtue over generations.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome tastes of disciplined restraint after a period of painful reduction. Da Xu's flavor is not one of explosive release but of powerful containment -- like a dam holding back a great river. The election result produces a government or leader characterized by accumulated authority and stored potential rather than immediate dramatic action. There is a sense of "great things held in reserve," as if the real consequences of this election will unfold slowly over time rather than bursting forth immediately.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Three travelers on a road, one falling away so the remaining two can walk in balance -- the political field narrowing to its essential duality.
- A mountain growing while a lake shrinks -- resources flowing upward from the people to the state, willingly but not without cost.
- A single thunder beneath the earth (Fu) -- the first tremor of renewal stirring underground, invisible but inevitable.
- Good horses in pursuit (Da Xu imagery) -- powerful forces being trained, disciplined, and channeled rather than running wild.
- Two simple bamboo bowls used for offering (Sun's counsel of "two bowls suffice") -- the election decided not by lavish promises but by modest, sincere offerings.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The primary warning comes from the paradox of the third line: alliances of three are unstable and must shed a member. Any three-way political contest will inevitably reduce to two, and the manner of that reduction is the critical turning point. The surprise is the emergence of Da Xu as the changed hexagram -- after all the talk of decrease and sacrifice, the ultimate outcome is one of great accumulation. What appeared to be a story of loss transforms into a story of stored power. The mutual hexagram Fu warns that cyclical return is inevitable: whatever political force the electorate thought it had left behind is returning, and this return is the hidden engine driving the entire election.
