EVENT: Cape Verde Parliamentary Election (May 17)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 61 風澤中孚 (Zhong Fu / Inner Truth) -> CHANGED: 59 風水渙 (Huan / Dispersion) (Line 1)
BODY=巽(木), FUNCTION=兌(金)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The image is of wind blowing over a lake -- the breath of sincerity rippling across open water. Zhong Fu (Inner Truth) is the hexagram of genuine trustworthiness, its structure hollowed in the center like an empty vessel or an open heart. Two solid lines enclose two broken lines at the core, creating a shape that resonates with the image of an egg -- empty within, fertile, holding potential. The wind (Xun) above and the lake (Dui) below create a dialogue between gentle penetration and joyous openness. The first line's movement transforms the lower trigram from lake (Dui) into water (Kan), yielding Huan (Dispersion) -- the wind now scatters what had been gathered, blowing across water rather than resting upon a lake.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Xun (Wood) is acted upon by the function trigram Dui (Metal/Gold). Metal cuts wood: the active force threatens the body. The joyous, expressive lake-energy carries within it a blade that can harm the gentle, penetrating wind-energy. Trust (Zhong Fu) is offered in a context where the body is vulnerable to the very force it engages with.
- The first line is the foundation, the earliest and most tentative position. Its yao text speaks of preparation and alertness: "虞吉，有它不燕" (being prepared brings good fortune; having other concerns brings unease).
- Huan (Dispersion) as the changed hexagram introduces the king arriving at the ancestral temple -- the scattering of what was accumulated, but also the reconstitution of meaning through ritual and spiritual authority.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The first line reads: "虞吉，有它不燕" -- "Preparedness brings good fortune; if there are other concerns, there will be no peace." This is a counsel of singular focus: trust works only when attention is undivided. The moment one hedges or looks elsewhere, the inner truth dissipates. The transformation into Huan (Dispersion) reveals what happens when that focus wavers -- the gathered trust scatters like wind over water. But Huan is not purely negative; its counsel is that a king can reconstitute unity by going to the temple, by invoking something larger than any faction. The narrative arc moves from careful, focused trust-building to the dispersal of that trust into broader currents, requiring spiritual or symbolic authority to reconsolidate what was scattered.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the taste of something precious that nearly dissolves but is held together by an appeal to shared identity. It is the feeling of wind whipping across open water -- exhilarating, destabilizing, but ultimately purifying. The scattering (Huan) removes what is stale and rigid, and if a unifying center can be invoked -- the temple, the shared ancestor, the common purpose -- the dispersal becomes renewal. There is fragility here, but also authenticity: what survives the wind is genuinely rooted.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Wind rippling over a lake -- sincerity expressed through gentle, persistent influence that reaches everywhere.
- A hollow vessel or empty egg -- the open center that makes resonance possible, trust as an empty space rather than a solid mass.
- Pigs and fish (豚魚) -- even the lowliest creatures are moved by genuine sincerity; truth penetrates to every level.
- Wind scattering leaves across water -- the dispersal of accumulated trust, forcing renewal.
- A king entering the ancestral temple -- the invocation of shared identity to reconstitute what the wind has scattered.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The central warning is encoded in the five-element dynamic: metal cuts wood. The body is vulnerable, and the trust being offered may be met with a sharp, cutting response from the very force it seeks to engage. The first line's counsel of undivided focus ("having other concerns brings unease") warns that any division of attention will accelerate the dispersal. The surprise is that the scattering itself may be beneficial -- Huan's wind clears away stagnation and creates the conditions for a fresh start. But this only works if there is a spiritual or symbolic center strong enough to serve as a rallying point after the dispersal.
