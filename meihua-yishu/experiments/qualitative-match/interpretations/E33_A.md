1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The hexagram of the Marrying Maiden depicts an arrangement that is structurally unequal from the outset -- a younger sister given in marriage alongside the elder, occupying a subordinate role within a predetermined hierarchy. The energy here is one of accommodation within constraint, of accepting a lesser position in order to participate in a larger arrangement. Thunder over lake creates a volatile pairing: impulsive action above, receptive pleasure below. The five-element relationship favors the 體 side decisively, as metal (兌) overcomes wood (震), suggesting that the party represented by 體 holds structural advantage and the capacity to shape the outcome.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The fifth line -- "帝乙歸妹，其君之袂，不如其娣之袂良，月幾望，吉" -- introduces the image of a royal marriage where the attendant's sleeves are finer than the principal's. This points to a situation where the ostensibly secondary figure actually possesses superior qualities, and the moon approaching fullness suggests timing is nearly perfect. There is a hidden reversal of visible rank and actual capability.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with the Marrying Maiden's fundamental tension: a formal arrangement that appears disadvantageous or subordinate. But the fifth line transforms this narrative. The fact that the attendant outshines the principal suggests that someone entering from a seemingly weaker position carries unexpected strength. The transformation into Pure Joy (兌為澤) signals that the resolution dissolves the hierarchy entirely into mutual satisfaction. With 體 克 用, the outcome favors the established or structurally advantaged side, and the process moves from constraint to a surprisingly harmonious conclusion.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of unexpected pleasantness emerging from an initially awkward or constrained arrangement. What begins as a subordinate position resolves into genuine satisfaction. There is a sense of the moon reaching its fullness -- not quite there yet, but almost, and the approaching completeness itself is auspicious.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A royal bride whose attendant outshines her -- the secondary figure carrying the real brilliance.
- The moon nearly full -- timing almost perfect, illumination approaching its peak.
- Thunder over a lake becoming pure lake -- turbulence settling into calm, reflective pleasure.
- Sleeves of silk -- outward appearance revealing inner quality despite formal rank.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is in the fifth line's reversal of expectation: in a structure defined by hierarchy, the subordinate position proves advantageous. The Marrying Maiden hexagram overall warns "征凶，无攸利" -- advancing aggressively is ill-favored -- yet the transformation into Joy suggests that accepting the arrangement as given leads to the best outcome. The warning is against forcing a change in the established order; the reward comes from working within it gracefully.
