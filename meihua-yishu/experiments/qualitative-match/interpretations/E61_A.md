EVENT: Colombia Presidential Election First Round (May 31)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 32 雷風恆 (Heng / Duration) -> CHANGED: 34 雷天大壯 (Da Zhuang / Great Strength) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: (derived from primary)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram depicts thunder above wind -- the eldest son and eldest daughter in enduring union, the image of persistence and constancy. Heng speaks of what endures, what maintains itself through time without faltering. The judgment says "亨，无咎，利貞，利有攸往" -- prosperous, no disaster, favorable to be correct, favorable to have somewhere to go. The body trigram is Zhen (thunder/wood), representing initiative, movement, and decisiveness. The use trigram is Xun (wind/wood), representing penetration, gradual influence, and communication. Both trigrams belong to the wood element: body and use are of the same nature, neither helping nor harming the other. This is a situation of fundamental sameness, where the forces involved share the same character.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Zhen (wood) represents the subject -- active, initiating, with the character of spring growth and sudden movement.
- The use trigram Xun (wood) represents the interacting force -- also wood, but expressed as gentle penetration rather than sudden thunder. Same substance, different expression.
- The first line (初六) is the moving line, at the very bottom of the hexagram. Its yao text reads: "浚恆，貞凶，无攸利" -- "Seeking depth in duration from the start, even correctness brings misfortune, nothing favorable." This is a stark warning: trying to establish deep permanence at the very beginning, before foundations are laid, leads to misfortune. The line demands too much too soon.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with Duration, but the first line subverts the hexagram's positive character by insisting on depth before the time is ripe. "浚恆" is the image of drilling down to bedrock when you have barely broken ground -- an overreach of ambition at the inaugural moment. The transformation into Da Zhuang (Great Strength) shifts the scene dramatically: thunder now rises above heaven rather than above wind. Four yang lines push upward with irresistible momentum. Great Strength speaks of overwhelming force and peak power, but its judgment merely says "利貞" -- favorable only if correct. The movement from Heng to Da Zhuang suggests that the attempt to establish lasting depth is replaced by a surge of raw power. What was meant to be enduring becomes merely forceful.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the flavor of premature ambition giving way to brute strength. The initial impulse to create something lasting is thwarted by its own impatience, and what emerges instead is a display of power that may not have enduring roots. The taste is of vigor without patience, force without the depth that the first line tried and failed to establish. Da Zhuang's "ram butting the fence" (羝羊觸藩) lurks as a possibility -- great strength that does not know when to stop, charging into barriers.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Drilling for a deep well on the first day of construction -- seeking permanence before the surface has even been cleared, demanding too much too soon.
- Thunder above wind becoming thunder above heaven -- the gentle, penetrating partner replaced by the overwhelming sky, intimacy giving way to grandiosity.
- A ram lowering its horns -- Great Strength's signature image of powerful forward charge, unstoppable but potentially reckless.
- Two wooden instruments playing the same note -- body and use both being wood, creating resonance but also redundancy, sameness without complementary tension.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The first line's "貞凶" (correctness brings misfortune) is the central warning: even doing the right thing in the wrong sequence produces bad results. Seeking depth and permanence at the outset, before circumstances allow it, is counterproductive. The surprise is that a hexagram about endurance transforms into one about peak strength -- suggesting that the failure to establish patience leads to a compensatory eruption of force. The wood-wood dynamic means there is no external element providing either support or challenge; the situation is self-referential, lacking the creative tension that different elements provide. The overall caution is against conflating intensity with durability.
