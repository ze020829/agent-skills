EVENT: NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs First Round (Apr 18-May 5)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 35 火地晉 (Jin / Progress) -> CHANGED: 12 天地否 (Pi / Standstill) (Line 5)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Fire rises above the earth -- the sun ascending over the horizon, illuminating everything beneath it. Jin is the hexagram of progress and advancement, of a loyal vassal receiving recognition and horses from the king, granted audience three times in a single day. The fundamental image is of merit being rewarded, of upward mobility earned through genuine service. The bright fire of achievement shines visibly over the broad earth of the realm. Yet the fifth line, at the sovereign position, introduces a transformation that dramatically reverses this ascending trajectory.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The Ti trigram is Kun (earth/soil), the subject -- broad, receptive, patient, capable of supporting great endeavors. The Yong trigram is Li (fire), the external environment -- brilliant, attention-commanding, illuminating. Fire generates earth (fire produces ash/soil): Yong生Ti, meaning the external environment actively nourishes the subject. This is a favorable dynamic where the surrounding brilliance feeds and strengthens the subject's foundation. The fifth line stands at the apex of the fire trigram, the brightest point of illumination.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The fifth line reads: "悔亡，失得勿恤，往吉，无不利." Regret vanishes. Do not worry about loss or gain -- going forward is auspicious, nothing is unfavorable. This is an extraordinary statement of liberation from anxiety: stop calculating, stop worrying, simply advance. Yet the transformation into Hexagram 12, Pi (Standstill/Stagnation), is jarring. Heaven rises above earth, each moving in its own direction, communication severed. The process thus moves from open progress and rewarded merit toward a condition where exchange ceases and each party retreats into isolation.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries a complex flavor: initial liberation and carefree advancement that gradually hardens into separation and non-communication. There is something bittersweet here -- the fifth line's "勿恤" (do not worry) rings with genuine freedom, yet the destination is Pi, where nothing flows between parties. The resolution feels like a celebration that quietly empties, guests departing one by one until the hall stands silent. The glory was real, but it does not sustain connection.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- The sun rising over the earth -- brilliant, warming, illuminating all achievements below.
- A vassal granted horses and triple audience -- merit recognized visibly and generously by authority.
- Heaven and earth separating -- what was connected becomes disconnected, communication severed.
- A city wall crumbling into its moat (城復于隍) -- structures that once protected now collapsing inward.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The primary warning is the dramatic reversal from Jin to Pi: progress that transforms into stagnation. The fifth line's counsel to stop worrying about gains and losses may be wise in the moment but cannot prevent the structural drift toward disconnection. Fire nourishing earth is genuine, but the changed hexagram shows heaven and earth pulling apart -- the nourishment ceases when the parties no longer engage. The surprise is how quickly an atmosphere of open advancement can crystallize into closed isolation, suggesting that the peak of progress is precisely the moment when stagnation begins to form.
