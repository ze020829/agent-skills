HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 22 賁 (Bi / Adornment) -> CHANGED: 36 明夷 (Ming Yi / Darkening of the Light) (Line 6)
MUTUAL: 3 屯 (Zhun / Difficulty at the Beginning)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation of surface beauty and decoration -- fire at the base of the mountain, illuminating the slopes with color and pattern. The energy is aesthetic, ornamental, concerned with how things appear rather than what they fundamentally are. But the changing line at the top position -- the final, culminating position -- transforms this adornment into a darkening of the light, a dramatic shift from brilliance to obscurity.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The mountain illuminated by fire below -- a grand visual display where light plays across a massive, immovable surface, creating patterns of shadow and color. The light is the active force; the mountain is the passive canvas.
- The top line reads: "白賁，无咎" -- white adornment, no fault. This is the moment where decoration is stripped away to its essence: pure, unadorned white. The most elaborate display ends in simplicity.
- The mutual hexagram Zhun (Difficulty at the Beginning) reveals that beneath the ornamental surface, there is the struggle of something trying to be born -- a sprout pushing through frozen soil. The inner reality is one of difficult emergence, not effortless beauty.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins as a display of brilliance -- light playing across surfaces, creating visual drama and aesthetic impact. The emphasis is on appearance: how things look matters more than what they are. As the hexagram progresses upward through its lines, the adornment becomes increasingly refined until, at the top line, it reaches "white adornment" -- the most austere form of beauty, where all color is drained away to leave only light itself. Then comes the dramatic transformation into Ming Yi (Darkening of the Light): the brightness that was adorning everything suddenly goes beneath the earth, hidden, injured, suppressed. This is the trajectory from maximal display to maximal concealment. The mutual hexagram Zhun suggests that the whole process is underlain by the difficulty of something new struggling to emerge -- this display is not mere vanity but accompanies a genuine birth struggle. The turning point is the top line's "white adornment": the moment of ultimate simplification before the light goes dark.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a dramatic, almost theatrical quality -- a vivid display of light that climaxes in its own disappearance. It is not a failure but a natural cycle: brilliance reaching its peak and then withdrawing into darkness. The flavor is bittersweet and visually striking, like watching a sunset that becomes more beautiful precisely as it vanishes. The resolution feels complete in the sense that the arc from light to dark is fulfilled, but emotionally it leaves a residue of loss or nostalgia -- the beauty was real but temporary.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Fire illuminating the base of a mountain at night -- dramatic interplay of light and shadow, color emerging from darkness, then returning to it.
- White, unadorned radiance -- the moment when all artificial color is stripped away and only pure light remains, just before it disappears entirely.
- A bright bird (明) descending into the earth (夷) -- light being swallowed or hidden by the ground beneath.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The unexpected element is the speed and completeness of the transition from adornment to darkness -- the hexagram moves from elaborate beauty to concealed light in a single step. The mutual hexagram Zhun warns that what looks like a purely visual phenomenon actually contains within it the difficult labor of something new being born, obscured by the spectacle on the surface.
