EVENT: SpaceX Starship Flight 12 Block 3 Launch
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 62 小過 (Xiao Guo / Small Exceeding) -> CHANGED: 32 恆 (Heng / Duration) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 28 大過 (Da Guo / Great Exceeding)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation where something small overreaches slightly -- thunder above the mountain, a small bird flying too high. The energy is one of minor excess: pushing just a bit beyond what is proportional or expected, but not catastrophically so. The kua text says explicitly: "可小事，不可大事" -- small matters may proceed, great matters should not. There is a ceiling on what can be accomplished. The flying bird imagery ("飛鳥遺之音，不宜上，宜下") suggests that upward ambition is penalized; downward modesty is rewarded.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- A small entity or effort that exceeds its station slightly -- not a dramatic overreach but a minor one. Like a bird that flies a bit too high and loses its song on the wind.
- The second line reads: "過其祖，遇其妣，不及其君，遇其臣，无咎" -- passing by the grandfather and meeting the grandmother, not reaching the ruler but meeting the minister, no fault. This describes a situation of partial attainment: not reaching the highest target but connecting with a secondary one, and this is acceptable.
- The mutual hexagram Da Guo (Great Exceeding) reveals a startling inner reality: while the surface shows only small overreach, the internal structure bears the stress of a ridgepole bending under excessive weight (棟橈). There is far more structural strain beneath the surface than the exterior suggests.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation presents itself as modest -- a small excess, nothing to be alarmed about. The second line reinforces this: you don't reach the king, but you do meet his minister, and that is fine. The transformation into Heng (Duration/Constancy) at line 2 suggests that this partial attainment solidifies into something lasting -- the second line of Heng reads simply "悔亡" (regret vanishes). What was achieved, even though it fell short of the ultimate target, becomes a stable foundation. However, the mutual hexagram Da Guo warns that the internal reality is more extreme than the external presentation. The ridgepole is bending; the structural limits are being tested in ways not immediately visible. The process unfolds from small overreach to stabilized partial achievement, but beneath the surface, the stress is considerable. The turning point is whether the partial connection (minister instead of king) is accepted gracefully or whether frustration at the shortfall leads to further pushing -- which the hexagram explicitly warns against.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a modest, workmanlike quality -- not the grand achievement hoped for, but a respectable partial result that endures. It is the flavor of "good enough" solidifying into permanence. The transformation into Heng gives it durability rather than brilliance. It feels like settling into a reliable rhythm after an ambitious attempt. There is a quiet tension between the modest surface result and the significant structural stress that produced it -- the effort was greater than the outcome suggests.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A small bird flying higher than it should, its song trailing away in the wind -- effort that slightly exceeds its natural range.
- Passing the grandfather's shrine but meeting the grandmother; not reaching the king but finding the minister -- a journey that arrives at a secondary destination.
- A ridgepole bending under weight (from the mutual Da Guo) -- hidden structural strain beneath an outwardly modest situation.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram's explicit warning is directional: "不宜上，宜下" -- upward ambition is penalized, downward humility is rewarded. The surprise from the mutual hexagram Da Guo is the hidden severity of the structural stress -- what looks like a minor overshoot on the surface conceals a situation where the load-bearing capacity is being pushed to its limits.
