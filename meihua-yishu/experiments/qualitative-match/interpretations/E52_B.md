EVENT: Met Gala 2026
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 38 火澤睽 (Kui / Opposition) -> CHANGED: 10 天澤履 (Lu / Treading) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents fire above and lake below -- two elements that move in opposite directions, fire rising and water sinking, creating a fundamental image of separation, divergence, and estrangement. Kui means opposition, the state where things that should unite are pulled apart. Yet the guaci tempers this: "小事吉" -- small matters are auspicious. Opposition is not total failure; it is a condition where modest achievements are possible even amid fundamental disagreement. The fifth line reads: "悔亡，厥宗噬膚，往何咎" -- regret disappears, one's kinsman bites through the skin, going forward what blame? This is the moment where opposition dissolves through an intimate, almost visceral act of reconnection.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram is Dui (lake/metal), representing joyful expression, outward display, and communication. The functioning trigram is Li (fire), which in the five-element cycle is controlled by metal through the intermediary cycle -- but more directly, fire melts metal. The functioning force threatens to overwhelm and transform the body, suggesting an intense, potentially destructive interaction between display and the fire that illuminates it.
- The fifth line occupies the sovereign position amidst opposition, the leader who must bridge the gap between divergent forces. The biting-through-skin imagery suggests that reconnection requires penetrating beneath surface differences.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The transformation into hexagram 10, Heaven over Lake (Lu / Treading), is a remarkable shift. Lu is the image of treading on a tiger's tail -- extreme danger navigated with grace and care. The guaci says "履虎尾，不咥人，亨" -- treading on the tiger's tail, it does not bite, success. The arc moves from the static opposition of fire and water to the dynamic, perilous act of walking alongside power without being destroyed. What was fundamental disagreement becomes a delicate negotiation with overwhelming force. The fifth line of Lu reads: "夬履，貞厲" -- decisive treading, correct but dangerous. The resolution demands boldness combined with perfect awareness of the danger.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of dazzling danger navigated with poise. The feeling is of walking a razor's edge between opposing forces -- fire and water, heaven and earth, tiger and human -- and emerging unscathed not through strength but through exquisite timing and grace. There is glamour in this danger, a quality of radiance that comes from proximity to something that could consume you. The tone is electric, carrying the thrill of a near-miss that becomes a triumph of composure.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Fire rising above a sinking lake -- two forces in fundamental opposition, moving away from each other, creating tension in the space between.
- A kinsman biting through skin -- the intimate, almost aggressive act of breaking through surface resistance to reach genuine connection.
- Treading on a tiger's tail -- the supreme image of walking alongside overwhelming power with total composure, one false step meaning destruction.
- Pigs splashed with mud, a cart full of ghosts -- the Kui imagery of seeing threats and grotesqueries that turn out to be illusions, enemies that are actually friends.
- Heaven above a joyful lake -- the transformed image of delight expressed within proper cosmic order.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The fire-threatens-metal dynamic means the body is genuinely at risk from the forces it engages with. Opposition (Kui) warns that appearances deceive -- the upper six line of Kui famously describes seeing pigs covered in mud and a carriage loaded with ghosts, only to discover that the perceived enemy is actually a marriage partner. The surprise in this reading is the transformation from static opposition to dynamic, thrilling navigation. The tiger does not bite. The key warning from Lu's fifth line -- "夬履，貞厲" -- is that decisive action is necessary but dangerous. One must be both bold and precise. Hesitation invites the tiger's jaws; recklessness invites them equally.
