EVENT: FA Cup Final 2026 (May 16)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 17 澤雷隨 (Sui / Following) -> CHANGED: 25 天雷无妄 (Wu Wang / Innocence) (Line 6)
BODY=震(木), FUNCTION=兌(金)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The image is of thunder submerged beneath a lake -- the dynamic, explosive force of Zhen held beneath the calm, joyous surface of Dui. Sui (Following) speaks of adaptability, of knowing when to yield and follow rather than lead. Thunder rests; the lake smiles. The entire structure suggests that the moment calls for responsiveness rather than initiative, for reading the current and moving with it. But the sixth line -- the culmination point -- transforms the lake into heaven, producing Wu Wang (Innocence/The Unexpected), a hexagram that speaks of acting without artifice and also of being struck by unforeseeable events.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Zhen (Wood) is acted upon by the function trigram Dui (Metal/Gold). Metal cuts wood -- this is a destructive relationship in which the function harms the body. The active, joyous surface energy attacks the vital, growing force beneath it. The body is under pressure, constrained, even threatened by the force it must follow.
- The sixth line of Sui is the most extreme position of following -- the point where following becomes binding: "拘係之，乃從維之" (bound and tied, then followed and secured). The king sacrifices at the Western Mountain.
- Wu Wang (Innocence) as the changed hexagram introduces heaven's thunder -- the spontaneous, uncalculated discharge of natural energy that cannot be anticipated or controlled.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The sixth line reads: "拘係之，乃從維之。王用亨于西山" -- "He is bound and tied, then followed and secured. The king makes offerings at the Western Mountain." This is an image of extreme commitment -- following carried to the point of physical constraint, where the follower is literally tied to what they follow. The sacrifice at the Western Mountain adds a ritual, ceremonial quality -- this binding is not casual but sacred. The transformation into Wu Wang then breaks this pattern completely. Where Sui was all about deliberate following, Wu Wang is about the eruption of the unplanned. Thunder under heaven crashes without warning. The narrative arc moves from willing submission and ritual binding to the sudden intrusion of something genuinely unexpected -- an innocent, uncalculated force that disrupts all strategies.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the sharp, electric taste of surprise after prolonged expectation. It is the feeling of careful plans suddenly overturned by an event no one could have predicted. Wu Wang's character is double-edged: innocence can mean purity and rightness, but it also means the "disaster of the innocent" (无妄之災) -- harm that comes to those who did nothing to deserve it. The flavor is of lightning striking a clear sky, of a result that cannot be explained by any strategy or preparation.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder sleeping beneath a still lake -- great power held in abeyance, following the calm surface.
- Ropes and bindings -- the follower tied to their commitment, unable to break free.
- A king sacrificing at a mountain shrine -- ritual devotion at the furthest, most solemn point of following.
- Thunder crashing from a clear sky -- the innocent, unexpected event that rewrites the story.
- A tethered ox taken by a passing stranger (无妄之災) -- loss that comes to those who did everything right.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The central warning is that metal cuts wood: the body (Zhen) is genuinely damaged by the function (Dui). Whatever joyous, attractive force is at work, it exacts a real cost on the vital, growing energy beneath it. The surprise -- encoded in Wu Wang -- is that the final outcome eludes all calculation. The sixth line's extreme binding gives way to heaven's thunder, which obeys no human plan. Wu Wang counsels: "其匪正有眚，不利有攸往" -- if not righteous, there will be calamity, and it is not favorable to go anywhere. The warning is to accept what comes without trying to force an outcome.
