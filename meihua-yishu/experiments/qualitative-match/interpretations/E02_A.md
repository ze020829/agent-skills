HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 37 家人 (Jia Ren / The Family) -> CHANGED: 53 漸 (Jian / Gradual Progress) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: 64 未濟 (Wei Ji / Before Completion)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation centered on domestic order, internal harmony, and the proper roles within an established household or system. Wind over fire -- the flame within the home, warmth radiating outward through proper channels. The energy is one of structured intimacy, where each part knows its place and contributes to the warmth of the whole. The changing line at the initial position introduces the theme of setting boundaries early, establishing the rules of the house before anything begins.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- A household or contained system with defined roles -- each member occupying a specific place, creating warmth through disciplined cooperation rather than spontaneous eruption.
- The first line reads: "閑有家，悔亡" -- establishing boundaries/gates within the family, regret vanishes. This is the figure of the gate-keeper, the one who sets the initial conditions that determine everything that follows.
- The mutual hexagram Wei Ji (Before Completion) reveals that beneath the orderly surface, nothing is actually finished. The internal reality is one of incompleteness -- the fox has almost crossed the river but its tail is still wet. There is a persistent gap between appearance and resolution.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins with a strong emphasis on containment and order -- the household is being set up, roles assigned, expectations clarified. The first line's message is clear: if you establish proper fences and boundaries at the outset, there will be no regret. The transformation into Jian (Gradual Progress) suggests that after this initial ordering, the situation evolves slowly and methodically, like a wild goose gradually ascending from water to rock to tree to hilltop. Nothing happens suddenly; each stage follows naturally from the last. However, the mutual hexagram Wei Ji warns that despite this orderly progression, the situation never quite reaches completion -- there is always one more step, one more adjustment needed. The turning point is the moment of initial boundary-setting: once the gates of the household are established, the gradual unfolding becomes inevitable, but completion remains perpetually just ahead.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a stately, processional quality -- measured, dignified, unhurried. It feels like watching something unfold according to a script that everyone follows faithfully, yet the final scene never quite arrives. The flavor is gradual warmth rather than sudden brilliance, domestic rather than dramatic, contained rather than explosive. It leaves a feeling of gentle incompleteness -- everything in its place, yet something still unresolved in the distance.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A well-ordered household where each member sits at the proper seat and the fire burns steadily within -- warmth through structure.
- Wild geese gradually ascending from shore to stone to tree branch -- each stage of elevation deliberate and measured.
- A small fox crossing a frozen river, its tail dipping into the water just at the end -- almost there, but not quite.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is that what appears to be about spectacle or external display is actually about internal ordering and domestic containment. The warning from Wei Ji is that the sense of completion this event seems to promise may be illusory -- something remains unfinished or unresolved beneath the orderly surface.
