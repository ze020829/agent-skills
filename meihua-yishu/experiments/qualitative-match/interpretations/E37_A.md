EVENT: Grand National at Aintree 2026 (Apr 11)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 3 水雷屯 (Zhun / Difficulty at the Beginning) -> CHANGED: 17 澤雷隨 (Sui / Following) (Line 4)
体用: 體=震(木), 用=坎(水). 用生體, 體方受益.

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Water above thunder: the image of Difficulty at the Beginning, a scene of primordial chaos where new life struggles to emerge through resistant terrain. The hexagram evokes a seedling pushing through frozen soil, a thunderstorm breaking over flooded ground -- "元亨利貞，勿用有攸往，利建侯" -- there is fundamental potential for success, but one should not rush forward; instead, establish a solid base. The five-element relationship is nurturing: water (坎) generates wood (震), meaning the very obstacles and dangers of the environment actively nourish the sprouting force. Difficulty feeds growth here.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The fourth line -- "乘馬班如，求婚媾，往吉，无不利" -- riding horses in formation, seeking alliance, advancing is auspicious, nothing unfavorable. This is the one thoroughly positive line in an otherwise cautious hexagram. Where most lines counsel restraint, the fourth breaks free: the horses are moving, the alliance is sought, and the advance is blessed. The transformation into Following (隨) introduces the principle of adaptive response -- "元亨利貞，无咎" -- supreme success through going with the flow.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins in tremendous difficulty -- the primordial struggle of new energy fighting to emerge through hostile conditions. Rain, thunder, confusion, false starts. But the fourth line marks a decisive pivot: from hesitation to committed advance. The horses move in formation, which suggests not a lone charge but a coordinated push. The request for alliance (求婚媾) implies that success comes through partnership, not solitary effort. The transformation into Following confirms that the resolution lies in adaptive responsiveness -- reading the changing conditions and moving with them rather than against them. Water nourishing wood means the very chaos and danger of the situation provides the energy for breakthrough.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the exhilarating flavor of emergence from chaos -- the moment when the seedling breaks through, when the horses find their rhythm after a stumbling start. It tastes of a difficult beginning giving way to forward momentum, of confusion resolving into a clear direction to follow. There is something joyous and relieved in it, the lake (澤) of Following replacing the danger (坎) of the beginning.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Horses riding in formation through a storm -- coordinated movement through chaotic conditions.
- A seedling breaking through frozen ground -- new life emerging from tremendous resistance.
- Thunder beneath rain -- explosive energy trapped under dangerous water, seeking release.
- Seeking alliance on horseback -- the quest for partnership as the key to forward movement.
- Lake replacing water -- danger transforming into joyful openness.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the fourth line's unqualified positivity within a hexagram otherwise defined by difficulty and caution. While most of Zhun counsels waiting and establishing foundations, line four says: go now, seek your alliance, advance without hesitation. This suggests a narrow window of opportunity within the broader difficulty -- a moment when conditions briefly align for decisive forward action. The warning from Following is in its fourth line: "隨有獲，貞凶" -- following that yields gain can be dangerous if one is not sincere. Success through adaptability must be grounded in genuine purpose, not opportunistic shifting.
