EVENT: Mortal Kombat 2 Opening Weekend (May 15)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 33 天山遯 (Dun / Retreat) -> CHANGED: 31 澤山咸 (Xian / Influence) (Line 6)
BODY=艮(土), FUNCTION=乾(金)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The primary image is of strategic withdrawal -- heaven rising above the mountain, the great pulling away from the solid and grounded. Dun speaks of a time when retreat is not weakness but wisdom, when the powerful recognize that the moment favors disengagement rather than confrontation. The mountain (Gen) stands firm below while heaven (Qian) ascends ever higher above it, creating a widening gap. Yet the sixth line transforms this retreat into something unexpected: Xian (Influence/Sensing), the hexagram of mutual attraction and emotional resonance, where the lake rests atop the mountain and moisture condenses into feeling.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Gen (Mountain/Earth) is nourished by the function trigram Qian (Metal/Gold). Metal enriches earth, so the active force supports the passive structure. The grounded, solid element receives genuine benefit from the powerful, creative force above it.
- The sixth line sits at the very top of the hexagram -- the position of culmination and reversal. Its movement transforms heaven into lake, shifting the dynamic from austere withdrawal to sensory engagement.
- Xian as the changed hexagram introduces the theme of emotional connection -- feeling in the body, sensation moving through limbs, the interplay between stillness and responsiveness.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The sixth line of Dun reads: "肥遯，无不利" -- "Fat retreat, nothing unfavorable." This is the most luxurious, most complete form of withdrawal -- departing with abundance, leaving nothing behind, suffering no loss in the departure. The "fatness" of this retreat suggests that whoever withdraws does so from a position of strength, not desperation. The transformation into Xian then adds a fascinating layer: the withdrawal opens a space for a new kind of connection. Xian's yao text for the top line -- "咸其輔頰舌" (influence at the jaws, cheeks, and tongue) -- suggests that what emerges is primarily verbal, expressive, communicative. The influence is at the level of speech and surface impression rather than deep structural change.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries a quality of elegant detachment followed by superficial but pleasurable engagement. It is the feeling of stepping back from a crowded arena into a quieter space where one can finally feel and respond. The "fat retreat" ensures comfort; the transformation into Xian provides stimulation. But because the influence reaches only the mouth and tongue (the top line of Xian), the connection remains at the level of words, impressions, and surface appeal rather than deep transformation.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A wealthy traveler departing a bustling city with full coffers -- retreat as abundance, not deprivation.
- A mountain catching mist from a lake -- the moment when stillness becomes receptive to feeling.
- Words on lips and tongues -- influence that operates through speech, expression, and sensory impression rather than through force.
- Heaven ascending beyond the mountain peak -- the powerful withdrawing to a place beyond ordinary reach.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the pivot from withdrawal to sensory engagement. One expects Dun to end in silence and distance, but the transformation into Xian opens a channel of feeling and expression. The warning from Xian's upper line is that influence limited to mouth and tongue can be shallow -- all talk and little substance. The "fat retreat" is genuinely favorable, but what follows may be more seductive than substantive.
