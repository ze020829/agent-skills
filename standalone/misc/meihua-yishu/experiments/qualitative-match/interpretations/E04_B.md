HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 57 巽 (Xun / The Gentle, Wind) -> CHANGED: 53 漸 (Jian / Gradual Progress) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 38 睽 (Kui / Opposition)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation of gentle, persistent penetration -- wind upon wind, doubled Xun. The energy is one of subtle influence that works through repetition and insinuation rather than force. The kua text notes: "小亨" -- small success is possible, not great success. The doubled wind image suggests that the same subtle force is applied and then re-applied, layer upon layer, working its way into cracks and openings through persistence rather than power.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The wind -- invisible, intangible, but penetrating. This is not a force that confronts directly but one that enters through every available opening, bending what it cannot break. Doubled, it represents a sustained campaign of subtle influence that never lets up.
- The second line reads: "巽在床下，用史巫紛若，吉，无咎" -- Xun/submission beneath the bed, using diviners and shamans in profusion, auspicious, no fault. This is a peculiar image: humbling oneself to the point of hiding under a bed, while deploying intermediaries (史巫 -- scribes, diviners, ritual specialists) in great numbers to communicate or persuade. The approach is indirect, working through agents and messengers rather than through direct confrontation.
- The mutual hexagram Kui (Opposition) reveals a fundamentally conflicted inner nature: fire and lake moving in opposite directions, divergence and disagreement at the core. Despite the gentle exterior, the internal reality is one of parties looking away from each other, unable to find common ground.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins with a posture of extreme deference and subtlety -- someone crouching beneath the bed, sending out waves of intermediaries to make their case. The approach is anything but direct; it works through channels, through ritual specialists, through the language of divination and legitimacy. The transformation into Jian (Gradual Progress) at line 2 suggests that this indirect approach eventually yields slow, methodical advancement -- like wild geese ascending from water to rock to tree, one stage at a time. However, the mutual hexagram Kui (Opposition) warns that the internal dynamics are fundamentally divisive. Even as the gentle approach gradually advances, the parties involved are looking in opposite directions, disagreeing on essentials. The hexagram suggests the process will be characterized by elaborate indirectness, multiple intermediaries, and gradual forward motion, but the core disagreement is never truly resolved -- only papered over by procedure and ritual. The turning point is whether the profusion of intermediaries (史巫紛若) can create enough procedural momentum to overcome the underlying opposition.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a slow, procedural, almost bureaucratic quality -- progress through channels, through forms, through the proper motions rather than through decisive action. The transformation into Jian (Gradual Progress) reinforces the unhurried pace: each stage follows the last according to protocol. The flavor is one of incremental advancement within a framework of persistent underlying disagreement. It does not feel resolved; it feels managed. The result is partial and processual rather than conclusive -- movement without arrival.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Someone hiding beneath a bed while sending out scribes and diviners to plead their case -- extreme deference combined with a profusion of indirect advocacy.
- Wind entering through every crack, invisible but relentless -- a force that never confronts but never stops.
- Wild geese ascending gradually from water to rock to branch (from Jian) -- measured, dignified progress through established stages.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The unexpected element is the combination of extreme surface gentleness (doubled Xun) with fundamental inner opposition (mutual Kui). What appears to be a harmonious, gradual process conceals a deep structural disagreement that the gentle approach cannot actually resolve. The warning is that the profusion of intermediaries and procedures may create an illusion of progress while the core conflict remains untouched, like wind blowing across the surface of a lake without disturbing the stones at the bottom.
