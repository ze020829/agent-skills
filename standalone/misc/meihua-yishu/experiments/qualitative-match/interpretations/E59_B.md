HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 8 水地比 (Bi / Holding Together) -> CHANGED: 2 坤為地 (Kun / The Receptive) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: (derived from primary)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents the image of water resting upon earth -- a natural, harmonious relationship where water gathers in the low places the earth provides. Bi speaks of alliance, union, and mutual support. Its judgment is auspicious: "吉。原筮元永貞，无咎" -- auspicious, from the original divination, great and enduring correctness, no disaster. The body trigram is Kun (earth), representing receptivity, stability, and the capacity to support. The use trigram is Kan (water), representing danger, flow, and adaptability. In the five-element cycle, earth controls water -- the body restrains the use element. This is a favorable configuration where the subject holds structural advantage over the forces it engages.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Kun (earth) represents a broad, stable, accommodating foundation -- an entity with deep reserves of patience and the capacity to absorb and contain what comes to it.
- The use trigram Kan (water) represents the flowing, adaptive forces that seek alliance and gather upon the earth's surface. Water controlled by earth means these forces are channeled and contained rather than running wild.
- The fifth line is the sovereign position, and its yao text reads: "顯比，王用三驅，失前禽，邑人不誡，吉" -- "Manifest holding-together. The king uses the three-sided hunt, letting the forward game escape. The people of the town are not warned. Auspicious." This is the image of a generous, open-handed leadership that does not trap everything but deliberately lets some escape, ruling through magnanimity rather than total control.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with Holding Together -- a situation of alliance, gathering, and mutual support under a central figure. The fifth line's image of the three-sided hunt is striking: the king hunts but leaves one side open, allowing prey to flee if they choose. This is governance through attraction rather than coercion, power that holds together by being generous enough to release what does not wish to stay. The transformation into Kun (Pure Earth) represents the complete realization of the receptive principle -- all six lines become yin, creating total stability, total receptivity, total groundedness. The movement is from selective alliance to universal accommodation. The earth absorbs everything, supports everything, refuses nothing.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of profound stability and inclusive support -- the earth that holds all things without discrimination. It tastes of quiet confidence, the kind of strength that does not need to assert itself because its very nature is to support. The three-sided hunt image gives the outcome a quality of deliberate openness, a choice to not maximize extraction but to build loyalty through generosity. The final state of Pure Receptive (Kun) suggests an entity that has become entirely foundational -- a platform, a ground, a base upon which others can build.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Water pooling in the hollows of the earth -- natural alliance, each element finding its place without force.
- A king hunting with three sides of the net, leaving the fourth open -- power exercised with deliberate restraint, allowing free exit, winning loyalty through clemency.
- The vast plain of pure earth -- Kun's final image of total receptivity, the ground that supports all structures equally.
- A broad basin gathering streams -- the stable center that collects and holds what flows toward it.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The earth-controls-water dynamic is entirely favorable for the body, suggesting the subject maintains structural command of the situation. The surprise is in the fifth line's counsel of deliberate release -- "失前禽" (letting the forward game escape) suggests that the wisest strategy is not to capture everything but to demonstrate openness. The transformation into Pure Receptive (Kun) could be read as a warning against excessive passivity -- pure yin has no initiating force of its own, and Kun's guidance is "利牝馬之貞" (favorable for the mare's perseverance), counseling followership rather than leadership. The question is whether total receptivity represents the fulfillment of purpose or a surrender of initiative.
