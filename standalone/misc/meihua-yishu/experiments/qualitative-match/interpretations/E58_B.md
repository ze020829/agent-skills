MUTUAL: (derived from primary)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents the image of a great bronze cauldron -- fire above, wind (wood) below, the ancient vessel of transformation where raw materials are cooked into something refined and nourishing. The Ding is the supreme symbol of renewal after revolution, the vessel through which a new order is established and consecrated. Fire (Li) sits above as the body trigram, representing illumination, vision, and clarity. Wind/wood (Xun) feeds the fire from below as the use trigram. In the five-element cycle, wood generates fire: the use trigram directly nourishes and strengthens the body. This is a deeply favorable configuration where the active ingredient feeds and empowers the receiving structure.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Li (fire) represents brilliance, display, technological vision, and public illumination. It is the light that makes things visible.
- The use trigram Xun (wind/wood) represents penetration, subtle influence, communication, and the fuel that sustains the flame. Wood generating fire means the presented content actively strengthens and energizes the platform.
- The second line's yao text reads: "鼎有實，我仇有疾，不我能即，吉" -- "The cauldron has substance. My rival has illness and cannot approach me. Auspicious." This is a striking image of a vessel filled with real content while competitors are weakened and unable to challenge.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with the Cauldron full and potent. The second line confirms that the vessel contains genuine substance -- this is not empty ceremony but real nourishment. The rival's inability to approach suggests a competitive landscape where the presenting force holds a clear advantage because others are hampered or diminished. The transformation into Lu (The Wanderer) introduces a significant shift: from the settled, consecrated vessel to the figure of a traveler moving through unfamiliar territory. Fire moves from sitting above wood to sitting above mountain -- illumination now rests upon a high, isolated peak. The Wanderer's character is of small success ("小亨"), caution in foreign lands, and the vulnerability of one who has no permanent home. This suggests that despite the substance and competitive advantage of the cauldron phase, what follows is a period of movement, displacement, and the need for careful navigation in new terrain.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries a dual flavor: initial strength and substance giving way to a more precarious, exploratory phase. The cauldron phase tastes of consecration and genuine power, a vessel brimming with real offerings. But the wanderer phase introduces the taste of impermanence -- brilliance displayed on a mountaintop, visible to all but exposed to the elements. The overall sensation is of launching something genuinely powerful that then must prove itself across unfamiliar landscapes.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A bronze cauldron filled with offerings, steam rising -- the ancient ritual vessel brimming with substance, consecrating a new era.
- A rival weakened and unable to approach -- competitors sidelined by their own afflictions while the cauldron stands full.
- A traveler carrying a torch on a mountain pass -- brilliance in motion, illuminating but exposed, moving through terrain that is not yet home.
- Fire fed by wind -- the perfect synergy of fuel and flame, where communication and content energize the vision.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the transformation from the settled, powerful Cauldron into the rootless Wanderer. What begins as a position of substance and competitive dominance shifts into a phase requiring humility and caution. Lu warns: "旅貞吉" -- the traveler must maintain integrity to find good fortune. The yao text's image of a rival weakened by illness is favorable but also suggests that advantage comes partly from others' misfortune rather than solely from one's own merit. The wood-generates-fire relationship is genuinely nourishing, but fire on a mountaintop (Lu's image) can be both a beacon and a signal that attracts unwanted attention.
