EVENT: Peru Presidential Election
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 19 地澤臨 (Lin / Approach) -> CHANGED: 60 水澤節 (Jie / Limitation) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: 24 地雷復 (Fu / Return)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The primary hexagram Lin (Approach) depicts earth over lake -- a great force drawing near, descending toward what lies below. It carries the energy of authority approaching the people, of leadership coming down to govern, of a powerful presence making itself felt. The hexagram is inherently auspicious ("great success, beneficial to persist"), but it carries a stark warning: "at the eighth month, there will be misfortune." This temporal warning suggests that the favorable conditions of this approach are time-limited; what arrives with promise now contains within it the seeds of future difficulty. In an election context, this is a leader or political force approaching power with great initial momentum, but the clock is already ticking on how long this advantage will last.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The approaching force (two yang lines at the base growing upward) represents a rising political energy -- a candidate, movement, or coalition gaining strength and moving toward governance. The earth above is receptive, yielding ground to this ascending force.
- The mutual hexagram Fu (Return) again introduces the theme of cyclical renewal. The same first stirring of yang energy that characterizes Fu suggests this election is powered by the return of something -- a political ideology, a former leader's influence, or a popular sentiment thought to have been exhausted.
- The changed hexagram Jie (Limitation/Regulation) represents water over lake -- a system of measured restraint, of setting boundaries and establishing rules. Its image is one of regulated flow rather than unchecked expansion.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The election unfolds as an act of approach: a commanding political force draws near to power, descending upon the political landscape with apparent confidence and popular support. The fifth line of Lin reads: "知臨，大君之宜，吉" -- "Wise approach, befitting a great ruler, auspicious." This is the sovereign line, and its message is unambiguous: the figure at the center of this election approaches governance with wisdom and discernment, and this is precisely what a great ruler should do. The word "知" (wisdom, knowledge) is key -- this is not brute-force politics but an approach characterized by intelligence and strategic understanding. Through the mutual hexagram Fu (Return), the deeper current reveals itself: this wise approach is also a return, a coming-back of something that had receded. The combination of "wise approach" and "return" suggests a political figure or force that has learned from past failures and returns with greater sophistication. The transformation into Jie (Limitation) is telling: the expansive, approaching energy of Lin is channeled into a system of limits and regulations. The fifth line of Jie reads "甘節，吉" -- "Sweet limitation, auspicious." The outcome is one where the new governance embraces restraint not as bitter medicine but as a natural and even pleasant discipline.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the flavor of measured, wise governance replacing unregulated ambition. Jie's character is fundamentally about knowing when enough is enough -- establishing sustainable limits rather than pursuing unlimited expansion. The election produces a result that feels structured and deliberate rather than chaotic or revolutionary. There is sweetness in this limitation, suggesting the populace accepts the new boundaries willingly rather than having them imposed by force. The tone is mature and considered, like a river finding its proper channel.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A great figure descending from a height to stand among the people -- the imagery of Lin as authority approaching, making itself accessible and present.
- A wise ruler surveying the landscape before entering the gate -- the fifth line's "knowing approach" suggests careful reconnaissance before committing to governance.
- A single sprout of green pushing through frozen earth (Fu) -- the return of political vitality after a barren period.
- Water regulated by the banks of a lake (Jie) -- the new governance as a system of natural containment, where flow is permitted but excess is checked.
- The eighth month warning -- a shadow of future reversal hanging over present success, like autumn approaching at the height of summer.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The most striking warning is Lin's explicit time limit: "至于八月有凶" -- "reaching the eighth month, there is misfortune." Whatever favorable conditions this election establishes, they carry an expiration date. The approach that begins with wisdom and popular support will, by a specific future point, encounter serious difficulty. This is not a vague warning but a calendrical one -- the hexagram insists that decline is already programmed into the success. The surprise is the sweetness of the limitation (Jie): rather than experiencing the new governance as oppressive restriction, the texture of the outcome suggests willing, even grateful acceptance of boundaries. The mutual hexagram Fu, shared with interpretation A, reinforces the theme that this election is fundamentally about cyclical return -- whatever rises now has risen before, and will recede again.
