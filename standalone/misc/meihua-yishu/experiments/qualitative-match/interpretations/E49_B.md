EVENT: Devil Wears Prada 2 Opening Weekend
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 27 山雷頤 (Yi / Nourishment) -> CHANGED: 42 風雷益 (Yi / Increase) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram depicts a mountain standing over thunder -- the image of a mouth, with the upper jaw (mountain, still) and the lower jaw (thunder, moving). The entire structure is about nourishment: what feeds us, what we consume, and how we sustain ourselves and others. The guaci reads "觀頤，自求口實" -- observe nourishment, seek sustenance for yourself. The fifth line, the sovereign position, reads: "拂經，居貞吉，不可涉大川" -- going against the norm, but staying put and holding to correctness brings good fortune; one must not attempt to cross great rivers. This is a leader who nourishes others by unconventional means but must not overextend.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram is Zhen (thunder/wood), dynamic and initiating, representing growth and ambition. The functioning trigram is Gen (mountain/earth), which in the five-element cycle means earth is controlled by wood -- the body dominates the functioning force. This gives the body trigram the upper hand, suggesting that the core energy of the situation is strong and can overcome resistance.
- The fifth line is the position of authority, but here it must act by holding still rather than venturing outward. The nourisher-in-chief must stay home.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The transformation into hexagram 42, Wind over Thunder (Yi / Increase), is powerfully favorable. Yi means increase, benefit, gain -- the image of wind and thunder reinforcing each other, the upper diminishing itself to benefit the lower. The Yi guaci says "利有攸往，利涉大川" -- it is favorable to have a direction, favorable to cross great rivers. The arc therefore moves from careful, somewhat restricted nourishment into a period of genuine increase and expansion. What begins as a contained act of feeding and sustaining transforms into something that actively goes out and benefits broadly. The fifth line of Yi reads "有孚惠心，勿問元吉" -- with sincerity and a generous heart, do not even ask, it is supremely auspicious.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of generous abundance flowing outward. There is a sense of something that was carefully nurtured in a contained space now breaking free into wider benefit. The feeling is of a feast prepared behind closed doors that then opens to the public, nourishing far more than originally intended. The tone is warm, expansive, and marked by genuine generosity rather than mere spectacle.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A great mouth, mountain above and thunder below -- the primal act of chewing, of taking in and transforming raw material into sustenance.
- A leader who stays home to nourish -- the sovereign who feeds the realm not by venturing outward but by cultivating what is already at hand.
- Wind and thunder together -- two forces that amplify each other, creating a powerful wave of beneficial energy.
- Crossing great rivers -- the transition from cautious containment to bold, expansive action.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The fifth line's injunction "不可涉大川" creates an interesting tension with the changed hexagram's encouragement to cross great rivers. The message is sequential: first, stay put and nourish correctly; then, when the transformation completes, expansion becomes possible and even advisable. The warning is against premature overreach. The wood-dominates-earth dynamic means the core energy is strong, but the nourishment theme reminds us that strength must be properly fed and directed. The surprise is how dramatically the situation improves from careful restraint to active increase -- a trajectory from modest feeding to overflowing abundance.
