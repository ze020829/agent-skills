EVENT: F1 Miami Grand Prix 2026
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 17 澤雷隨 (Sui / Following) -> CHANGED: 45 澤地萃 (Cui / Gathering Together) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram shows a lake above thunder -- the image of thunder retreating beneath still water, the dynamic yielding to the receptive. Sui means "following," the act of adapting oneself to the movement of the time. The guaci reads "元亨利貞，无咎" -- supremely successful if correct, no blame. The first line reads: "官有渝，貞吉。出門交有功" -- the official undergoes change, correctness brings fortune; going out through the gate to make contacts brings achievement. This is the image of someone who changes course or role and finds success by engaging with the outside world.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram is Dui (lake/metal), which represents pleasure, communication, and outward expression. The functioning trigram is Zhen (thunder/wood), which in the five-element cycle is controlled by metal -- the body dominates the active force. This favorable dynamic means the core entity holds power over the initiating energy, able to direct and shape the action.
- The first line marks a transition point, where change in leadership or direction occurs at the very beginning, setting the tone for everything that follows.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The transformation into hexagram 45, Lake over Earth (Cui / Gathering Together), moves the scene from adaptive following to purposeful congregation. Cui is the image of water collecting on the earth's surface -- people gathering at a temple, resources pooling in one place. The guaci says "亨，王假有廟" -- success, the king goes to the ancestral temple. The arc moves from flexible adaptation to a focused act of assembly. What begins as responsive, opportunistic following crystallizes into a deliberate gathering of forces around a central point. The first line of Cui reads: "有孚不終，乃亂乃萃" -- sincerity that does not last, disorder then gathering. This suggests initial confusion before the gathering takes shape.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of diverse elements converging into a coherent assembly. The feeling is of scattered followers coalescing around a central figure or purpose, like tributaries joining a river. There is initial turbulence and uncertainty, but the final image is of purposeful aggregation. The tone is communal rather than individual -- the emphasis falls on the collective rather than any single actor. The gathering is not spontaneous but deliberate, requiring a powerful symbolic center (the temple, the king) to hold it together.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder retreating beneath a lake -- the dynamic force yielding to the still surface, adapting rather than asserting.
- An official who changes course and finds success -- the leader who pivots at the opening moment and benefits from engaging externally.
- Water pooling on flat earth -- the slow, inevitable gathering of dispersed elements into one place.
- A king at the ancestral temple -- the powerful center around which all scattered forces rally.
- Tributaries joining a river -- the merging of many small streams into one purposeful flow.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The metal-controls-wood dynamic gives the body a commanding position, but the Following hexagram counsels humility and responsiveness rather than domination. The surprise is in the first line's emphasis on change -- "官有渝" -- suggesting that the initial configuration is not the final one, and success comes through willingness to adapt at the very start. The transition to Gathering Together (Cui) warns that the initial phase may feel disordered ("乃亂乃萃"), but this chaos is the precursor to meaningful assembly. The key danger is sincerity that does not persist -- the gathering fails if commitment wavers after the initial enthusiasm.
