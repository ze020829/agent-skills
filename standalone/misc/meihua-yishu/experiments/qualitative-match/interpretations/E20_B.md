EVENT: European Council Spring Summit
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 26 山天大畜 (Da Xu / Great Accumulation) -> CHANGED: 22 山火賁 (Bi / Adornment) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 54 雷澤歸妹 (Gui Mei / The Marrying Maiden)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The mountain sits atop heaven, containing immense creative power within a structure of stillness and discipline. Hexagram 26, Da Xu, is the image of great accumulation -- vast resources, knowledge, and strength gathered and held in reserve. This is not a moment of expenditure but of storage; the energy of heaven (乾) is restrained by the mountain (艮), channeled and preserved rather than released. The judgment tells us: "利貞。不家食吉，利涉大川" -- it is favorable to maintain correctness; eating away from home is auspicious, and it is favorable to cross great rivers. There is encouragement to take accumulated wisdom into the world rather than hoarding it privately.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The mountain that restrains heaven represents an institutional structure with the authority and mass to contain enormous forces. This is governance, tradition, or an established order that holds creative and potentially disruptive energies in check through sheer weight and permanence.
- The mutual hexagram Gui Mei (The Marrying Maiden) introduces the dynamic of political alliance and asymmetric partnership -- arrangements made for strategic reasons, where one party yields to the terms of another for the sake of a larger union.
- The changed hexagram Bi (Adornment/Grace) shifts the emphasis from raw accumulation to aesthetic refinement -- the mountain now sits over fire rather than heaven, suggesting that the stored energy is being shaped into something culturally polished, decorative, and presentable rather than merely powerful.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with Da Xu's massive containment. Great resources and great tensions are held within a stable structure. At the second line, where the change occurs, the yao text is strikingly simple: "輿說輹" -- the cart has shed its axle. The vehicle has stopped moving; the wheels are disconnected from the chassis. This is enforced stillness -- not because the journey is over, but because the mechanism of forward motion has been deliberately or accidentally disengaged. The cart is loaded but cannot proceed. Through the mutual hexagram Gui Mei, we see that the underlying dynamic involves political marriages of convenience -- alliances formed not from love but from necessity, where unequal partners are bound together. The transformation into Bi (Adornment) then reveals the resolution: what cannot move forward in substance moves forward in form. The energy shifts from accumulation to decoration, from building reserves to polishing surfaces. "亨，小利有攸往" -- there is success, but only small advantage in moving forward. The emphasis falls on presentation rather than substance.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of stalled substance dressed in elegant form. The great accumulated power cannot move forward because the axle has been removed from the cart, so instead the energy is redirected into adornment, protocol, and aesthetic refinement. The flavor is one of impressive presentation masking a mechanical inability to advance -- the summit looks beautiful, the communique is well-crafted, but the vehicle that would carry real change forward is temporarily disabled. There is grace without momentum.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A massive cart loaded with treasure but missing its axle -- all the resources in the world, rendered immobile by a single missing connector.
- A mountain sitting on top of heaven -- the sheer institutional weight required to contain creative and potentially explosive forces.
- Fire beneath the mountain casting flickering light on stone walls -- the transformation of raw power into decorative illumination.
- A bride adorned for a political wedding she did not choose -- the arrangement looks splendid from the outside.
- A young bull fitted with a wooden yoke, trained but not yet working -- the image of disciplined power not yet deployed.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the stark simplicity of "輿說輹" -- the cart sheds its axle. For a hexagram of Great Accumulation, where enormous resources are gathered and the judgment promises the ability to cross great rivers, having the cart lose its axle is a deflating interruption. All that has been accumulated cannot be moved forward. The warning is that the shift to Bi (Adornment) may represent a consolation rather than a solution -- when substance cannot advance, there is a temptation to invest in appearance. The mutual hexagram Gui Mei further warns that alliances formed under these conditions serve political necessity but may lack genuine equity or depth.
