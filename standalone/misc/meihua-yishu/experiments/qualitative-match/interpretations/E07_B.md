EVENT: Champions League Round of 16 Draw
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 23 剝 (Splitting Apart) → CHANGED: 27 頤 (Nourishment) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: 2 坤 (The Receptive, Earth)

Changing line text (初六): 剝床以足，蔑貞凶
("Splitting apart the bed by the legs. Those who despise constancy meet misfortune.")

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram describes a situation of gradual erosion and dismantling from the bottom up. Mountain over Earth (艮 over 坤) — the mountain sits on eroding ground, its base being stripped away. Five yin lines have risen and only one yang remains at the top, about to be toppled. The energy is one of structural weakening, where the foundations are being eaten away and the remaining strength is precarious and isolated at the summit.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- An eroding force that works from the bottom upward — the first line shows the very beginning of this process: the bed's legs are being removed. This is not a sudden catastrophe but a systematic undermining. The foundation is attacked first.
- A solitary remaining strength (the single yang line at the top, 上九) that has not yet fallen but is increasingly isolated and unsupported as everything beneath it gives way.
- The mutual hexagram 坤 (Pure Earth) reveals that the interior of the process is entirely yin — receptive, passive, and lacking in active resistance. There is no internal force pushing back against the erosion.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins at its most vulnerable point: the first line, the very bottom, where the bed's legs are being split. This image is visceral — the structure you rest upon, the thing that supports you in sleep and vulnerability, is being dismantled starting with its foundation. The text warns that "those who despise constancy meet misfortune," suggesting that the erosion is connected to a failure to maintain principles or standards.

The changed hexagram 頤 (Nourishment, the Jaws) transforms the picture significantly. 頤 is Mountain over Thunder — open jaws that take in nourishment. The trajectory moves from destruction to a question of feeding and sustenance. What is being consumed? What is being nourished? The splitting apart ultimately leads to a reckoning about what truly sustains.

The mutual hexagram 坤 in the process phase means there is no resistance to the erosion during its middle stages — things simply give way. The turning point is when the splitting reaches its natural conclusion and the question shifts from "what is falling apart?" to "what can nourish what remains?"

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has a quality of foundational reckoning. Something that appeared stable is revealed to have been structurally unsound. The resolution is not about rescue or reversal but about honestly assessing what can be sustained going forward (頤). The feeling is sobering rather than catastrophic — the bed loses its legs, but the changed hexagram suggests that the focus shifts to nourishment and what is truly essential. It is a stripping-down-to-basics kind of outcome.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A bed whose legs are being sawed off in the night — the sleeper does not notice until they crash to the floor. Foundations fail before superstructures do.
- A mountain whose soil is slowly washing away in rain, exposing bare rock — what seemed solid turns out to have been held up by easily eroded material.
- Open jaws (頤) — the situation becomes about consumption and sustenance, about what feeds whom and at whose expense.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The hexagram warns that the erosion has already begun at the base and that dismissing the importance of foundational integrity ("despising constancy") accelerates misfortune. The surprise is that the process of splitting apart leads not to ruin but to a fundamental question of nourishment (頤) — destruction becomes a gateway to reconsidering what truly sustains the structure.
