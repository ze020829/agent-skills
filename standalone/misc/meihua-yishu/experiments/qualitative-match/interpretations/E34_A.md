EVENT: India Assam Legislative Assembly Election (Apr 9)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 26 山天大畜 (Da Xu / Great Accumulation) -> CHANGED: 9 風天小畜 (Xiao Xu / Small Accumulation) (Line 5)
体用: 體=乾(金), 用=艮(土). 用生體, 體方受益.

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Mountain above heaven: the image of Great Accumulation, where massive reserves of energy are held in check by the stillness of the mountain. This hexagram speaks of gathering strength before a great venture -- "不家食吉，利涉大川" -- it is auspicious not to eat at home, meaning the accumulated power is meant for public service, not private consumption. The five-element relationship is highly favorable to 體: earth (艮) generates metal (乾), meaning the institutional structure, the containing force, actively nourishes and strengthens the principal actor. This is a situation of being well-supported by one's environment.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The fifth line -- "豶豕之牙，吉" -- the tusks of a gelded boar, auspicious. This is a vivid image of dangerous force that has been neutralized at its source. The boar retains its tusks but has lost its aggressive drive. The transformation into Small Accumulation (小畜) shifts from great reserves to modest ones -- "密雲不雨" -- dense clouds that have not yet released rain. A key force here is restraint that has been successfully internalized rather than externally imposed.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc moves from massive accumulation to something more modest but still intact. The fifth line suggests that the decisive moment involves the defanging of a threat -- not by removing the weapon but by removing the will to use it. The transformation from Great to Small Accumulation suggests a downsizing of ambition or scope, but not a defeat. With 用 generating 體, the containing structures and supporting institutions actively feed the principal's strength throughout. The narrative is of someone who enters with enormous reserves and emerges with a smaller but more refined position, having neutralized opposition along the way.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome tastes of clouds gathering but rain not yet falling -- potential that remains suspended. There is accomplishment in restraint, a sense that what has been accumulated is preserved rather than spent. The feeling is of a strong position maintained rather than a dramatic breakthrough achieved.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A gelded boar's tusks -- danger defused at the source, weapons without aggression.
- Dense clouds over the western suburbs -- resources gathered but not yet released.
- A mountain holding heaven in place -- enormous power contained by immovable stillness.
- Not eating at home -- accumulated strength directed toward public purpose.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise lies in the shift from Great to Small Accumulation: one might expect such massive reserves to produce an overwhelming result, but instead the outcome is more contained, more measured. The clouds do not rain. This suggests that the accumulated power may not fully manifest in this particular event -- the potential exceeds the expression. The warning is against expecting a dramatic release when the hexagram counsels continued patience and holding.
