EVENT: NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs First Round (Apr 18-May 5)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 3 水雷屯 (Zhun / Difficulty at the Beginning) -> CHANGED: 17 澤雷隨 (Sui / Following) (Line 4)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Water above thunder -- clouds gathering over the first stirrings of spring, rain and lightning mingling in a scene of primordial birth-struggle. Zhun is the hexagram of initial difficulty, of a sprout forcing its way through frozen soil, of creation at its most precarious and vital moment. Everything is in flux, nothing is settled, and the forces involved are simultaneously chaotic and full of potential. The image is of a world being born, where each step forward requires enormous effort and the path ahead is obscured by storm clouds.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The Ti trigram is Zhen (wood/thunder), the subject -- dynamic, explosive, bursting upward with the urgency of new life. The Yong trigram is Kan (water), the external environment -- dangerous, flowing, deep, representing both peril and nourishment. Water generates wood in the productive cycle: Yong生Ti, meaning the external environment, for all its dangers, fundamentally feeds and sustains the subject. The difficulty is real, but the underlying dynamic is generative -- the storm waters nourish the struggling sprout even as they batter it.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The fourth line reads: "乘馬班如，求婚媾，往吉，无不利." Riding horses hesitantly, seeking alliance -- going forward is auspicious, nothing is unfavorable. After the overwhelming difficulty of the opening, the fourth line offers a decisive turning point: seek partnership, reach out for alliance, and the path opens. The transformation into Hexagram 17, Sui (Following), confirms this shift -- from struggling alone against primordial chaos to finding rhythm in adaptive responsiveness, from forced breakthrough to willing cooperation. Sui's "元亨利貞" promises fundamental success through flexibility.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of hard-won partnership emerging from initial chaos. The beginning is genuinely difficult -- disorienting, rain-soaked, uncertain -- but the resolution reveals that the struggle was the very thing that prepared the ground for meaningful alliance. There is grit in this outcome, the taste of mud and effort, but also the warmth of hands clasped in mutual dependence. The process of following (Sui) feels like finding one's rhythm after a stumbling start, the moment when disparate forces learn to move together rather than against each other.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A sprout breaking through frozen ground -- life at its most vulnerable and most determined.
- Storm clouds massing over thunder -- the mingling of danger and vitality at a moment of creation.
- Horses advancing hesitantly, seeking alliance -- the cautious approach toward partnership that transforms the situation.
- The lake resting above thunder (Sui) -- joyful responsiveness born from the willingness to follow the moment's lead.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the fourth line's unambiguous optimism within a hexagram defined by difficulty: "往吉，无不利" -- going forward is auspicious, nothing is unfavorable. This is the most encouraging statement possible, arriving precisely when the situation seems most chaotic. The warning comes from Zhun's fundamental nature: "勿用有攸往，利建侯" -- do not rush forward recklessly, but focus on building a foundation. The alliance-seeking of the fourth line succeeds because it is collaborative rather than aggressive. Those who charge ahead alone will be swallowed by the storm; those who seek partnership will find the storm nourishing.
