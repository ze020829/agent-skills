EVENT: SpaceX Starship Flight 12 V3 Launch (Apr-May)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 14 火天大有 (Da You / Great Possession) -> CHANGED: 26 山天大畜 (Da Xu / Great Accumulation) (Line 4)
MUTUAL: (derived from primary)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents fire blazing above heaven -- the sun at its zenith illuminating all below. Da You is the hexagram of supreme abundance, great harvest, and magnificent possession. Its judgment is simply "元亨" -- fundamentally prosperous. A single yin line in the fifth (sovereign) position presides over five yang lines, embodying the principle of one yielding ruler harmonizing many strong forces. The body trigram is Qian (heaven/metal), representing creative power, strength, and authority. The use trigram is Li (fire), representing brilliance, visibility, and illumination. Fire overcomes metal in the five-element cycle: the use element actively pressures the body. Despite the hexagram's auspicious overall character, the five-element dynamic introduces tension -- the illuminating force taxes the structural core.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Qian (metal) represents raw creative power, authority, and structural strength -- the engine and framework of the endeavor.
- The use trigram Li (fire) represents visibility, spectacle, and illumination. Fire melting metal means the very brilliance of the display places strain on the underlying structure.
- The fourth line's yao text reads: "匪其彭，无咎" -- "Not boasting of abundance, no disaster." This is counsel of restraint at the moment of great possession: do not swagger, do not inflate. The line occupies the position of the minister, close to the sovereign, where caution and modesty are essential.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The arc begins with Great Possession at its fullest -- fire illuminating heaven, resources abundant, everything visible and prosperous. The fourth line counsels against arrogance at this moment of plenty: "匪其彭" -- do not be puffed up. The transformation into Da Xu (Great Accumulation) shifts the image dramatically: fire above heaven becomes mountain above heaven. The blazing illumination is replaced by the stillness of a mountain containing heaven's power beneath it. Da Xu is about restraining great force, accumulating energy rather than expending it, storing rather than displaying. Its judgment says "利貞。不家食吉，利涉大川" -- favorable to be correct, not eating at home is auspicious, favorable to cross the great river. The trajectory moves from spectacular display to disciplined containment, from showing everything to storing power for future deployment.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the dual character of brilliance transitioning to restraint. The initial phase tastes of magnificent display -- the sun at noon, everything illuminated, great resources on show. But the changed hexagram introduces the flavor of mountain stillness, of deliberately banking the fire and accumulating reserves. The overall sensation is of a powerful demonstration that leads not to continued exhibition but to strategic storage and preparation. Da Xu's "good horse pursuit" (良馬逐) in its third line suggests that what is accumulated has genuine quality and speed, held in check until the moment is right.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- The sun blazing at its zenith above a clear sky -- maximum illumination, total visibility, nothing hidden.
- Fire above heaven becoming mountain above heaven -- the transition from radiant display to quiet, massive containment.
- A young bull fitted with a yoke before it grows horns ("童牛之牿") -- the wisdom of restraining great force early, before it becomes uncontrollable.
- A treasury being filled after a great harvest -- the movement from celebration of abundance to disciplined saving.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The fire-overcomes-metal dynamic is the key tension: the spectacular, visible element actively pressures the structural core. Great Possession is magnificent but the very act of display is costly to the underlying framework. The fourth line's counsel against boasting is both practical and prophetic -- overconfidence at this juncture invites structural stress. The transformation into Great Accumulation suggests that the wisest path forward is not continued spectacle but strategic restraint. The surprise is that such an auspicious primary hexagram leads to a changed hexagram about stopping and storing rather than advancing and displaying. The message is: the harvest is real, but wisdom lies in what you do after the harvest, not in the harvest itself.
