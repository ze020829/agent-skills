EVENT: FA Cup Final 2026 (May 16)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 13 天火同人 (Tong Ren / Fellowship) -> CHANGED: 49 澤火革 (Ge / Revolution) (Line 6)
BODY=離(火), FUNCTION=乾(金)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
The image is of fire blazing beneath heaven -- a great bonfire visible across the open field, drawing people together from all directions. Tong Ren (Fellowship) speaks of unity forged through shared purpose, of companions gathering under a vast sky. The fire burns openly, concealing nothing, and the heaven above it creates a sense of limitless space for this gathering. Yet the sixth line -- the very top, the point of culmination and excess -- transforms heaven into lake, turning Fellowship into Revolution (Ge). What begins as comradeship ends as upheaval; the gathering becomes the site of transformation.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram Li (Fire) is acted upon by the function trigram Qian (Metal/Gold). Metal and fire exist in a destructive relationship: fire melts metal, and metal can smother fire. The body (fire) overcomes the function (metal), suggesting that the receptive, illuminating force ultimately prevails over the hard, creative force -- but at the cost of burning through something valuable.
- The sixth line of Tong Ren occupies the most remote position -- "fellowship in the outskirts" (同人于郊). This is the furthest reach of community, the edge where belonging thins into isolation.
- Ge (Revolution) as the changed hexagram brings the image of animal skins being shed, of old orders overthrown, of the calendar turning.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The sixth line reads: "同人于郊，无悔" -- "Fellowship in the outskirts, no regret." This is a curious, understated conclusion. Rather than a grand climax of unity, the fellowship extends to its outermost boundary -- the suburbs, the margins -- and finds no regret there, but also no celebration. The transformation into Ge (Revolution) then introduces a dramatic shift: "己日乃孚" -- "On the ji day, there is trust." Revolution requires time to be believed; the change does not take hold immediately but must be confirmed through the passage of days. The arc moves from open fellowship to a marginal, quiet departure, and then into genuine structural change. The revolution that emerges is not a violent rupture but an organic transformation arising from the edges of community.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the taste of unexpected transformation emerging from apparent quietude. What seemed like a simple gathering on the open field turns out to be the staging ground for a fundamental change of order. The flavor is of controlled fire -- the same flame that drew people together now melts the old vessels and reforges them. There is a sense of inevitability: the fellowship was always heading toward this revolution, but no one noticed until the sixth line tipped over the edge.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- A bonfire on the outskirts drawing distant travelers -- fellowship at its furthest reach, where commitment is tested.
- Animal skins being stripped and replaced -- the old identity shed for a new one.
- Fire melting metal vessels -- the illuminating force overcoming the rigid, established structure.
- The ji day arriving -- a specific moment when trust crystallizes and the change becomes real.
- A field at the edge of town -- the place where belonging meets wilderness, and transformation begins.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise is the revolution emerging from fellowship's quietest corner. The sixth line is "no regret" but also no fanfare -- it is the figure who left the party early and sparked a revolution by doing so. The warning from Ge is clear: "征凶，居貞吉" (from the top line) -- aggressive action is dangerous; staying centered and principled is what secures the good outcome. The revolution succeeds not through force but through the patient accumulation of trust. Fire dominates metal here, but if the fire burns too hot, it destroys rather than transforms.
