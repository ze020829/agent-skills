EVENT: Benin Presidential Election (Apr 12)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 11 地天泰 (Tai / Peace) -> CHANGED: 19 地澤臨 (Lin / Approach) (Line 3)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents the image of heaven and earth in harmonious exchange -- the creative force descends, the receptive force rises, and the two meet in the middle to produce a condition of universal flourishing. Tai is the hexagram of peace, prosperity, and open communication between high and low. The fundamental picture is of a system functioning as it should: power flows downward to nourish, trust flows upward in return. Yet the third line, at the very boundary between inner and outer, introduces a crucial caveat about the cyclical nature of this prosperity.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The Ti trigram is Kun (earth/soil), representing the subject -- receptive, broad, nurturing, capable of absorbing and sustaining. The Yong trigram is Qian (heaven/metal), representing the external force or counterparty. Metal generates soil in the productive cycle: Yong generates Ti, meaning the external environment actively supports and nourishes the subject. This is a fundamentally favorable configuration -- the subject receives strength from outside forces without needing to struggle.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The third line reads: "无平不陂，无往不復。艱貞无咎，勿恤其孚，于食有福." No plain without a slope, no departure without a return. This is the pivotal wisdom: at the very height of peace, the text reminds us that reversal is inherent in all things. The transformation into Hexagram 19, Lin (Approach), depicts two yang lines advancing from below -- a figure of authority approaching, overseeing, arriving at a position of influence. Lin's hexagram text warns "至于八月有凶" -- the good fortune has a time limit. The process moves from established peace toward active governance, from passive flourishing to deliberate engagement.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome carries the flavor of a prosperous transition managed with awareness. It is not the ecstasy of sudden victory but the steady warmth of a system that works, a harvest gathered in good order. There is deep nourishment here -- "于食有福," blessings in food and sustenance -- suggesting material prosperity and genuine well-being. Yet underneath runs a sober recognition that this golden period will not last forever, lending the outcome a quality of purposeful gratitude rather than complacent celebration.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Heaven descending and earth rising to meet -- the powerful stooping down, the humble rising up, creating perfect exchange.
- No plain without a slope -- the landscape itself reminding us that flat ground inevitably gives way to inclines.
- A ruler approaching from above to oversee the realm -- authority arriving with benevolent intention.
- Food and blessings -- the simplest, most tangible form of prosperity, sustenance shared openly.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The core warning is temporal: Lin's "至于八月有凶" explicitly states that the favorable period has an expiration. The prosperity depicted here is real but not permanent. The third line's position at the threshold between inner and outer trigrams marks the exact point where peace begins its inevitable tilt toward change. The surprise may be how smooth and nourishing the process feels despite this awareness -- there is no crisis, only the gentle reminder to harvest while the season permits.
