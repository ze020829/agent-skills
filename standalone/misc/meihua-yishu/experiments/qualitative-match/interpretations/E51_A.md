EVENT: F1 Miami Grand Prix 2026
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 55 雷火豐 (Feng / Abundance) -> CHANGED: 62 雷山小過 (Xiao Guo / Small Exceeding) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: N/A

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
This hexagram presents thunder above fire -- the image of a great thunderstorm illuminated by lightning, a spectacle of maximum brightness and maximum sound occurring simultaneously. Feng means abundance, fullness, the zenith. The guaci says "亨，王假之，勿憂，宜日中" -- success, the king reaches this state, do not worry, it is fitting at midday. This is the moment of high noon, the sun at its peak, when shadows disappear and everything is visible. The first line reads: "遇其配主，雖旬无咎，往有尚" -- meeting one's matching lord, even for ten days no blame, going forward is esteemed. This is the auspicious encounter at the beginning of a grand moment.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The body trigram is Zhen (thunder/wood), dynamic, explosive, surging upward with aggressive energy. The functioning trigram is Li (fire), which in the five-element cycle is generated by wood -- wood feeds fire. This means the body exhausts itself to fuel the active display. The spectacle burns bright because the core energy is being consumed.
- The first line represents the initial encounter, the opening moment, where two matched forces recognize each other and the stage is set for the full spectacle.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The transformation into hexagram 62, Thunder over Mountain (Xiao Guo / Small Exceeding), is a striking deflation. From the blazing abundance of Feng, the scene shifts to a small bird flying too high -- the Xiao Guo guaci says "可小事，不可大事。飛鳥遺之音，不宜上，宜下" -- small matters succeed, great matters do not; the flying bird leaves only its cry; do not go up, go down. The arc moves from maximum spectacle and abundance to a situation where only modest achievements are possible, where overreaching is punished and the counsel is to stay low. The first line of Xiao Guo reads simply: "飛鳥以凶" -- the flying bird brings misfortune. What began as an auspicious encounter at the height of splendor becomes a warning against flying too high.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the flavor of a grand spectacle that resolves into something smaller and humbler than expected. The midday sun gives way to a bird's shadow crossing a mountain. There is the sense of diminution -- not catastrophe, but a scaling down, a recognition that the peak moment cannot be sustained and that what follows must be measured in smaller units. The tone shifts from thunderous brilliance to the quiet counsel: stay low, do small things well, do not reach for the sky.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder and lightning together -- a storm of maximum sensory intensity, the sky split by simultaneous light and sound.
- High noon, no shadows -- the moment when everything is fully illuminated and nothing can hide.
- Meeting one's matched lord -- two forces of equal stature recognizing each other at the opening moment.
- A small bird flying too high -- the creature that exceeds its natural limits and is warned to descend.
- A bird's cry fading as it flies beyond reach -- the echo of ambition that has overshot its mark.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The wood-feeds-fire dynamic is the key structural warning: the body is consumed by the spectacle it creates. Abundance (Feng) always carries the shadow of "盛極必衰" -- what peaks must decline. The surprise is the sharpness of the transition: from the king at midday to a small bird cautioned against flying upward. The first line begins auspiciously with a matched encounter, but the transformation suggests this initial promise leads not to sustained greatness but to a situation where only small, careful actions succeed. The flying bird of Xiao Guo serves as a vivid warning: do not mistake the opening brilliance for a mandate to soar indefinitely.
