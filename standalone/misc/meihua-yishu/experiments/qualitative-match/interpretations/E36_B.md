EVENT: Djibouti Presidential Election (Apr 10)
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 57 巽為風 (Xun / The Gentle Wind) -> CHANGED: 53 風山漸 (Jian / Gradual Progress) (Line 2)
体用: 體=巽(木), 用=巽(木). 體用同屬, 比和之象.

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Wind upon wind: the doubled image of gentle, persistent penetration. Xun is the hexagram of the soft force that enters everywhere -- through cracks beneath doors, through the canopy of a forest, through the subtle channels of influence that bypass direct confrontation. "小亨，利有攸往，利見大人" -- small success, favorable to advance, favorable to see the great person. The five-element relationship is one of perfect equivalence: both 體 and 用 are wood (巽), creating a situation of mutual reinforcement without tension. There is no conflict here, no overcoming -- only the same element reflecting itself, amplifying its own nature.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
The second line -- "巽在床下，用史巫紛若，吉，无咎" -- the wind penetrates beneath the bed, using diviners and shamans in abundance, auspicious, no fault. This is an image of deep, hidden influence operating through intermediaries and ritual specialists. The power here works not through visible authority but through the invisible channels of spiritual or behind-the-scenes influence. The transformation into Gradual Progress (漸) introduces the image of geese advancing step by step -- "鴻漸于磐，飲食衎衎，吉" -- the wild goose progresses to the rock, eating and drinking contentedly.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The narrative unfolds as a process of utterly gentle, patient, almost invisible advancement. Double wind creates a field of continuous soft pressure that meets no resistance because it provokes none. The second line reveals that this influence operates below the surface -- beneath the bed, through intermediaries, through ritual channels rather than political ones. The transformation into Gradual Progress confirms the pace: this is not a sudden seizure of power but a methodical, step-by-step progression where each advance is consolidated before the next begins. The wild goose finds its rock and feeds contentedly -- security through incremental positioning.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the quality of something arriving so gradually that its completion seems inevitable rather than dramatic. It tastes of wind that has worn down stone over decades -- the result is visible, but no one witnessed the moment of change. There is contentment in the final state, the settled satisfaction of the goose on its rock, well-fed and secure.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Wind beneath the bed -- influence operating in the most intimate, hidden spaces.
- Diviners and shamans in abundance -- power exercised through spiritual and ritualistic intermediaries.
- A wild goose progressing to the rock -- methodical, natural advancement finding secure footing.
- Double wind -- the same gentle force reinforcing itself endlessly, wearing down all resistance.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The surprise here is the absence of any dramatic turning point or crisis. The entire hexagram speaks of gentle, persistent influence that never confronts and therefore never fails. This can be read as either profoundly effective or profoundly static -- the same process continuing without interruption. The warning from Xun is subtle: "頻巽，吝" (line 3, not the moving line but present in the hexagram's character) -- excessive submissiveness becomes regrettable. The gentle approach can shade into passivity if carried too far. The comparison of 體 and 用 being identical elements suggests a situation where there is no real opposition, no creative tension -- everything moves in the same direction, which can mean either perfect harmony or perfect stagnation.
