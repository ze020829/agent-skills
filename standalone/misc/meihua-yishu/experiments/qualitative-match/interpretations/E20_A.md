EVENT: European Council Spring Summit
HEXAGRAM LABEL: A
PRIMARY: 51 震為雷 (Zhen / Thunder) -> CHANGED: 54 雷澤歸妹 (Gui Mei / The Marrying Maiden) (Line 2)
MUTUAL: 39 水山蹇 (Jian / Obstruction)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Double thunder -- shock upon shock, tremor following tremor. Hexagram 51, Zhen, presents a situation of sudden, alarming upheaval that demands immediate attention. Thunder upon thunder means the disturbance is not a single event but a repeated pattern, a rolling series of shocks that keeps everyone on edge. The original judgment captures this perfectly: "震來虩虩，笑言啞啞，震驚百里，不喪匕鬯" -- the thunder arrives with fear and trembling, yet afterwards there is composed laughter; the shock reaches a hundred miles, but the one presiding does not drop the sacrificial spoon and chalice. The essential image is of maintaining ritual composure under conditions of extreme disturbance.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The presiding figure who must maintain calm and ritual order despite the rolling thunder -- a host or chair who holds the ceremonial vessel steady while the ground shakes beneath them.
- The mutual hexagram Jian (Obstruction) reveals the structural difficulty underlying the situation: water before the mountain, paths blocked in certain directions while others remain passable. The gathering faces real impediments that cannot be willed away through mere resolve.
- The changed hexagram Gui Mei (The Marrying Maiden) introduces a dynamic of unequal partnership and obligatory alliance -- a younger sister given in marriage, an arrangement that serves political necessity more than natural affinity. The outcome involves accepting a subordinate or constrained role within a larger arrangement.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation opens amid repeated shocks. Thunder follows thunder, and the assembly must find its footing on trembling ground. At the second line, where the change occurs, the yao text reads: "震來厲，億喪貝，躋于九陵，勿逐，七日得" -- the thunder comes with danger, there is a great loss of valuables, and one must climb to the nine hills for safety. Do not pursue what is lost; in seven days it will be recovered. This is remarkable counsel: accept the loss, retreat to high ground, and trust that what was forfeited will return of its own accord. Do not chase after it. The process moves through Jian, confirming that the path forward is genuinely obstructed -- some directions are favorable ("利西南") while others are blocked. The transformation into Gui Mei then reveals the nature of the resolution: an arranged marriage, a political union born of necessity rather than desire. "征凶，无攸利" -- the maiden's own judgment warns that aggressive pursuit brings misfortune and there is nothing advantageous in forcing the issue. The alliance must be accepted on its own imperfect terms.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of a forced accommodation following a period of shock and loss. Something valuable is sacrificed in the turbulence, and rather than fighting to recover it immediately, the parties must retreat and wait. The eventual resolution takes the form of a political marriage -- an arrangement that binds parties together out of necessity, where one partner accepts a less-than-ideal position for the sake of collective stability. The flavor is pragmatic rather than triumphant, marked by compromise and the acceptance of asymmetry.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder rolling across a hundred miles, shaking every household -- a disturbance so large that no one within range can ignore it.
- Climbing to the nine hills with empty hands, having lost one's treasures in the panic -- strategic retreat to high ground.
- A sacrificial chalice held steady by trembling hands -- the discipline of maintaining ceremony when everything shakes.
- A younger sister given in marriage to seal an alliance -- the personal subordinated to the political.
- Water pooling before an impassable mountain, seeking the easier southwestern route.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The most striking element is the counsel not to pursue what is lost: "勿逐，七日得." In a moment of crisis, the instinct is to chase after what has been forfeited, but the hexagram insists on patience -- seven days, a full cycle, and the loss restores itself. The surprise is that the resolution takes the form of Gui Mei, an arranged and somewhat disadvantageous union. Those expecting the thunder to resolve into clear skies and mutual triumph will find instead a pragmatic arrangement that serves necessity. The warning from Gui Mei's own judgment -- "征凶，无攸利" -- is that any attempt to force a better outcome through aggressive action will make things worse.
