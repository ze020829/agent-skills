EVENT: FOMC March Interest Rate Decision
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 16 雷地豫 (Yu / Enthusiasm/Ease) -> CHANGED: 51 震為雷 (Zhen / Thunder) (Line 1)
MUTUAL: 39 水山蹇 (Jian / Obstruction)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Thunder erupts from the earth, and the ground trembles with the release of pent-up energy. Hexagram 16, Yu, depicts a moment of enthusiasm, readiness, and the marshaling of collective will. The single strong yang line in the fourth position leads the five yin lines -- a decisive figure commands the momentum, and the masses respond with eager alignment. The energy is preparatory and martial in nature: "利建侯行師" -- it is favorable to install leaders and mobilize armies. Yet the changing first line introduces an immediate note of danger within this enthusiasm.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- A dominant figure in the fourth position who generates enthusiasm and commands followership -- a conductor of collective energy who sets the tone and direction for the group.
- The mutual hexagram Jian (Obstruction) reveals that beneath the surface enthusiasm lies a landscape of genuine difficulty -- water over mountain, paths blocked, forward progress impeded. The enthusiasm is either a response to or a mask over real structural obstacles.
- The changed hexagram Zhen (Thunder doubled) represents the full unleashing of shock and tremor. Pure thunder upon thunder -- the event does not merely announce itself but reverberates, creating aftershocks that demand attention and recalibration.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The situation begins in Yu, where energy is mobilized with apparent confidence. But the first line, where the change occurs, carries a stark warning: "鳴豫，凶" -- trumpeting one's ease and enthusiasm aloud brings misfortune. This is the danger of premature celebration, of signaling confidence before the ground is secure. Someone at the lowest position makes noise about how favorable things are, and this very noise attracts disaster. The process then encounters the reality described by the mutual hexagram Jian: genuine obstruction, difficulty on the path ahead. Water pools before the mountain, and the way forward requires careful assessment of which routes are passable and which are blocked. "利西南，不利東北" -- some directions offer easier passage while others are firmly closed. The transformation into pure Zhen (Thunder) is dramatic: the single shock of enthusiasm becomes doubled thunder, a tremor that shakes everything. "震來虩虩，笑言啞啞" -- thunder comes with fear and trembling, but afterwards there is laughter. The shock is real and unsettling, but those who maintain composure through it emerge unscathed.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of a sudden, jarring shock that follows an initial period of overconfident ease. The flavor shifts dramatically from comfortable anticipation to startled alertness. There is a whiplash quality -- the mood turns from celebratory to anxious in an instant. Yet the doubled thunder of Zhen also carries a strange promise: after the initial terror, equilibrium returns, and those who kept their nerve find themselves laughing. The resolution is not smooth but percussive, not gradual but abrupt.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Thunder breaking from beneath the earth -- stored tension suddenly released, the ground itself seeming to move.
- A trumpeter sounding a premature victory call, only to attract enemy attention -- "鳴豫，凶."
- Water pooling at the base of a mountain, unable to flow forward -- the hidden obstruction beneath the surface confidence.
- Lightning striking the same place twice -- doubled thunder, Zhen upon Zhen, a shock followed immediately by another shock.
- A commander who rallies the troops with enthusiasm but discovers the road ahead is washed out.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The central warning is devastatingly specific: "鳴豫，凶" -- do not announce your confidence, do not trumpet your ease. The one who proclaims how favorable conditions are invites exactly the reversal they thought they had avoided. The surprise is the hidden presence of Jian (Obstruction) as the mutual hexagram -- what appears to be a moment of collective enthusiasm and readiness is actually underlain by genuine structural difficulty. The further surprise is the doubled thunder of the outcome: not one shock but two, suggesting that the initial disturbance may be followed by a second wave before stability returns.
