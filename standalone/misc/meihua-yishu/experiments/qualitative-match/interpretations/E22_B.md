EVENT: World Athletics Indoor Championships
HEXAGRAM LABEL: B
PRIMARY: 39 水山蹇 (Jian / Obstruction) -> CHANGED: 15 地山謙 (Qian / Modesty) (Line 5)
MUTUAL: 64 火水未濟 (Wei Ji / Before Completion)

1. SITUATION DESCRIPTION
Water atop the mountain -- danger pooling above stillness, the path forward blocked by a treacherous landscape. Hexagram 39, Jian, is the hexagram of obstruction and difficulty. The way ahead is genuinely impeded; this is not imagined trouble but real, structural impediment. The judgment offers directional counsel: "利西南，不利東北" -- the southwestern path (open and flat) is favorable, while the northeastern path (mountainous and steep) is not. The situation calls for recognizing which obstacles can be circumnavigated and which must be accepted as impassable. It is favorable to see a great person -- wisdom and leadership are needed to navigate the difficulty.

2. KEY ACTORS / FORCES
- The mountain standing in water represents an obstinate, immovable barrier confronted by flowing, adaptive danger. The competitors or participants face conditions that resist forward progress -- the terrain itself is hostile, and brute force will not overcome it.
- The mutual hexagram Wei Ji (Before Completion) reveals that beneath the surface difficulty lies a fundamental state of incompleteness. The small fox has almost crossed the river but risks wetting its tail at the last moment. Nothing has yet been brought to conclusion; everything remains in process, unfixed, and precarious.
- The changed hexagram Qian (Modesty) transforms the entire energy from struggle against obstruction to humble acceptance of one's place. The mountain hidden beneath the earth -- great capability concealed beneath a low profile. The earth does not boast of containing the mountain within it.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE
The narrative begins in genuine difficulty. Jian's landscape is forbidding: water and mountain create an impassable barrier in certain directions. The participants must choose their approach carefully. At the fifth line, where the change occurs, the yao text reads: "大蹇，朋來" -- great obstruction, but friends come to help. This is the pivotal moment: when the difficulty reaches its peak, when the obstruction seems most insurmountable, allies arrive. The fifth position is the sovereign line, and at this critical juncture, the leader in difficulty receives reinforcement from companions. The process moves through Wei Ji, confirming that completion has not yet been achieved -- the situation remains in flux, the river only partially crossed, every element still seeking its proper position. The transformation into Qian (Modesty) then reveals the nature of the resolution: "亨，君子有終" -- success, the noble person achieves completion. But this completion comes through modesty, through the willingness to lower oneself rather than assert dominance. The mountain goes beneath the earth; great strength is expressed through humility rather than display.

3. PROCESS NARRATIVE (continued)
The fifth line of Qian itself reinforces this theme: "不富以其鄰，利用侵伐，无不利" -- though not wealthy, one gains the help of neighbors; it is favorable to undertake campaigns, and nothing is unfavorable. Modesty here is not passivity but a strategic posture that attracts support and enables action that would otherwise be impossible.

4. QUALITATIVE OUTCOME CHARACTER
The outcome has the character of difficulty overcome through humility and the arrival of unexpected allies. The flavor is not one of solitary triumph but of collective effort -- the great obstruction is not smashed through by individual force but dissolved by the combined strength of friends who arrive at the critical moment. The resolution in Qian gives the outcome a quiet, understated quality: the mountain hidden beneath the earth does not announce itself. The greatest achievements here are expressed through restraint, through the willingness to keep great capability beneath a modest surface. The aftertaste is one of earned respect rather than flashy victory.

5. SPECIFIC IMAGERY / METAPHORS
- Water pooling at a mountain peak, creating a treacherous, slippery landscape that blocks all forward paths -- the stark reality of obstruction.
- Friends arriving at the moment of greatest difficulty -- "大蹇，朋來" -- the relief of reinforcement when one's own strength is exhausted.
- A small fox nearly across a frozen river, its tail dipping into the water at the last step -- Wei Ji's image of almost-but-not-quite completion.
- A mountain hidden entirely beneath flat earth -- immense strength and elevation concealed beneath an unassuming surface.
- A humble person whose neighbors voluntarily come to their aid, not because they are commanded but because they are respected.

6. WARNINGS / SURPRISES
The central surprise is the transformation from Jian (Obstruction) to Qian (Modesty) -- the resolution to profound difficulty is not breakthrough or conquest but humility. Those expecting the obstacle to be shattered will find instead that it is dissolved by a change in posture. The warning from Wei Ji as the mutual hexagram is that nothing is yet complete -- premature celebration or relaxation could cause the fox to wet its tail at the very last moment. The further surprise in the fifth line is that the greatest obstruction is also the moment when help arrives: "大蹇，朋來." The darkest hour summons companions, but only if one has cultivated the modesty and genuine character that makes others willing to come to one's aid.
